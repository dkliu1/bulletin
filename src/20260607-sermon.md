创世记 12:1–9 标志着圣经中的一个关键转折点。叙事焦点从创世记 1-11 章中悖逆人类的普世历史，转移到创世记 12 章中上帝借着一个人——亚伯兰（后来的亚伯拉罕）及其家族展开的特定救赎计划。在75岁（是的，75岁）的高龄，亚伯兰被呼召去牺牲他舒适的生活，走出舒适圈，走向一个充满未知与不确定因素的异国他乡。
Genesis 12:1–9 marks a critical turning point in the Bible, shifting the narrative focus from the universal history of a rebellious humanity from Genesis 1–11 to God’s specific plan of redemption in Genesis 12 through one man, Abram (later Abraham), and his family. At the age of 75, yes 75, Abram was called to sacrifice his comfortable life, to step out from his comfort zone, to travel ahead to an alien land, with lots of uncertainty and unknown situations.

    因着对上帝的顺服与信心，亚伯拉罕被应许获得三重福分与赏赐。他得到的应许包括：后裔（“我必叫你成为大国” —— 创世记 12:2），土地（“我要把这地赐给你的后裔” —— 创世记 12:7），以及福分（“我必赐福给你，叫你的名为大；你也要叫别人得福” —— 创世记 12:2）。

    Abraham was promised a three fold benefits and rewards, upon his obedience and faith on God. He was promised : Descendants (“I will make of you a great nation” — Genesis 12:2), Land (“To your offspring I will give this land” — Genesis 12:7), Blessing (“I will bless you and make your name great, so that you will be a blessing” – Genesis 12:2).

上帝不偏待人。上帝应许赐福给亚伯兰，并不是因为亚伯兰做了什么配得这福分的事，也不是单单为了亚伯兰自己的缘故：“我必赐福给你，叫你的名为大；你也要叫别人得福。”（创世记 12:2-3）
God doesn’t play favoritism. God promises blessing to Abram, not because Abram has done anything to deserve it. Neither is it for the sake of Abram himself: “I will bless you, and make your name great, so that you will be a blessing to others. (Genesis 12:2-3).

    亚伯拉罕蒙福是为了成为别人的祝福。这福分不是为了他自己，也不是为了他的家族；这福分是为了整个世界的缘故。亚伯拉罕和他的后裔要成为地上万族得福的管道。

    Abraham is blessed to be a blessing. The blessing is not for his sake, nor for the sake of his family; the blessing is for the sake of the whole world. Abraham and his descendants are to be the channel of blessing for all the families of the earth.

In Romans 4 Paul explains that Abraham wasn't saved by being perfect or following religious laws (which didn't even exist yet), but simply by trusting God's promise. Christians believe this exact same principle applies to Jesus' salvation: humans are saved by grace through faith in Christ, not by their own good deeds

When God made a covenant with Abraham, He promised that "through your offspring all nations on earth will be blessed" (Genesis 22:18). In Galatians 3:16, the Apostle Paul clarifies that this specific "offspring" refers to Jesus Christ. Jesus is the ultimate fulfillment of the promise made to Abraham, bringing spiritual salvation and forgiveness of sins to the entire world.

Because Jesus opened the door for salvation to the Gentiles (non-Jews), anyone who trusts in Jesus becomes a spiritual descendant of Abraham. Paul writes in Galatians 3:29, "If you belong to Christ, then you are Abraham’s seed, and heirs according to the promise." This means that salvation unites both Jews and Gentiles into one family of faith.
