# Psalm 110 | 诗篇 110

The LORD says to my lord: “Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet.”
耶和华对我主说：“你坐在我的右边，等我使你仇敌作你的脚凳。”  

The LORD will extend your mighty scepter from Zion, saying, “Rule in the midst of your enemies!”
耶和华必从锡安伸出你能力的杖来，说：“你要在仇敌中掌权。”  

Your troops will be willing on your day of battle. Arrayed in holy splendor, your young men will come to you like dew from the morning’s womb.
当你掌权的日子，你的民要以圣洁的妆饰为衣，甘心牺牲自己；你的民多如清晨的甘露。  

The LORD has sworn and will not change his mind: “You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek.”
耶和华起了誓，决不后悔，说：“你是照着麦基洗德的等次永远为祭司。”  
Isaiah 48:12-18 | 以赛亚书 48:12-18

“Listen to me, Jacob, Israel, whom I have called: I am he; I am the first and I am the last.
“雅各，我所选召的以色列啊，当听我言：我是耶和华，我是首先的，也是末后的。”

My own hand laid the foundations of the earth, and my right hand spread out the heavens; when I summon them, they all stand up together.
“我手立了地的根基，我右手铺张诸天，我一打发招呼，天地就一同侍立。”

“Come together, all of you, and listen: Which of the idols has foretold these things? The LORD’s chosen ally will carry out his purpose against Babylon; his arm will be against the Babylonians.
“你们当聚集而听，偶像之中谁曾说明这些事？耶和华所爱的人必向巴比伦行他所喜悦的事，他的膀臂也必加在迦勒底人身上。”  

I, even I, have spoken; yes, I have called him. I will bring him, and he will succeed in his mission.
“惟有我曾说过，主曾选召他，领他来，他的道路就必亨通。”

“Come near me and listen to this: “From the first announcement I have not spoken in secret; at the time it happens, I am there.” And now the Sovereign LORD has sent me, endowed with his Spirit.
“你们要就近我来听这话：‘我从头就没有在隐密处说话，自从有这事，我就在那里。’ 现在，主耶和华差遣我和他的灵来。”

This is what the LORD says—your Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel: “I am the LORD your God, who teaches you what is best for you, who directs you in the way you should go.
耶和华你的救赎主，以色列的圣者如此说：“我是耶和华你的神，教训你使你得益处，引导你所当行的路。”

If only you had paid attention to my commands, your peace would have been like a river, your well-being like the waves of the sea.
“甚愿你素来听从我的命令，你的平安就如河水，你的公义就如海浪。”

In the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God almighty. Holy Father, Holy Son, Holy Spirit, Holy Trinity. As a name of a church, St David’s Moorabbin has been very special for me especially for the past 11 months, when I suddenly got this urgent call from Bishop Paul Barker, I was immediately moved by the Holy Spirit, I said yes to this calling doing the mission of the Holy Son, and I committed myself to honor the Holy Father serving the saints here with all the Holy ministries.
奉圣父、圣子、圣灵的名。圣哉，圣哉，圣哉，主神全能者。圣父、圣子、圣灵，神圣三位一体。作为一间教会的名字，莫拉宾圣大卫堂（St David’s Moorabbin）对我来说非常特别，尤其是过去的11个月里。当我突然接到保罗·巴克（Paul Barker）主教的紧急呼召时，我立刻被圣灵感动，顺服了这一呼召，去履行圣子的使命，并致力于荣耀圣父，以各项圣工来服侍这里的圣徒。

Before July 2025, I didn’t know this St David’s or any St David’s at all. But I know a few Holy Trinity. Rick and I were married at Holy Trinity HK. My son Dietrich and his wife Lois were married at Holy Trinity Doncaster. And my first student placement as an ordinand was at Holy Trinity Balaclava, near St Kilda. Holy Trinity as a name of a church reminds us the theology, the reality, the family, the community, the unity of a God who is eternal, who is plural, who is communal.
在2025年7月之前，我根本不知道这间圣大卫堂或任何圣大卫堂。但我知道几间以“圣三一”命名的教会。我和里克（Rick）是在香港圣三一堂结婚的；我的儿子迪特里希（Dietrich）和他的妻子洛伊斯（Lois）是在唐卡斯特圣三一堂（Holy Trinity Doncaster）结婚的；而我作为待按手神学生的第一次实习是在圣基尔达附近的巴拉克拉瓦圣三一堂（Holy Trinity Balaclava）。“圣三一”作为教会的名字，提醒着我们关于神的真理：祂是一位永恒的、多元的、契合的、合一的神，祂拥有祂的教义、真实性、家庭、社群与联合。

Trinity could be understood as tri unity. Three persons, the Father, the Son, the Spirit are united as one God, one unit, one team, one family. Three persons, but all equal and all agreeable, they never compete, never fight, never disapprove one another. Three persons, always coordinate, always subordinate. A church is therefore seen after God, many individuals, but corporate, but one whole block, with no falling apart pieces.
三位一体（Trinity）可以理解为“三合一”（tri unity）。圣父、圣子、圣灵三个位格联合为一位神、一个单元、一个团队、一个家庭。三个位格，全然平等且全然和谐，祂们从不竞争，从不争吵，从不否定彼此。三个位格，永远相互协调，永远彼此顺服。因此，教会是以神的方式呈现的：虽有许多独立的个体，却是一个整体，是一个完整的联合体，没有一片四分五裂的碎片。  

Let us read Psalm 110 today with a Trinitarian and Christological lens.
让我们今天用三位一体和基督论的视角来阅读诗篇110篇。

The LORD says to my lord: “Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet.” God the Father invites God the Son (the Messiah) to sit at His right hand, establishing an eternal kingdom, while the Holy Spirit serves as the divine author revealing this eternal plan. In the original Hebrew, this reads as "Yahweh (the Lord, God the Father) says to my Adoni (my Lord, the Messiah)."
耶和华对我主说：“你坐在我的右边，等我使你仇敌作你的脚凳。” 父神邀请子神（弥赛亚）坐在祂的右边，建立一个永恒的国度，而圣灵则是启示这一永恒计划的神圣作者。在希伯来语原文中，这句话的意思是：“耶和华（主，父神）对我的阿多奈（我的主，弥赛亚）说。”
# Matthew 22:41-46 | 马太福音 22:41-46

While the Pharisees were gathered together, Jesus asked them, “What do you think about the Messiah? Whose son is he?” “The son of David,” they replied. He said to them, “How is it then that David, speaking by the Spirit, calls him ‘Lord’? For he says, “‘The Lord said to my Lord: “Sit at my right hand until I put your enemies under your feet.”’ If then David calls him ‘Lord,’ how can he be his son?” No one could say a word in reply, and from that day on no one dared to ask him any more questions.
法利赛人聚集的时候，耶稣问他们说：“论到基督，你们的意见如何？他是谁的子孙呢？” 他们回答说：“是大卫的子孙。” 耶稣说：“这样，大卫被圣灵感动，怎么还称他为主，说：‘主对我主说：你坐在我的右边，等我把你仇敌放在你的脚下’？大卫既称他为主，他怎么又是大卫的子孙呢？” 他们没有一个人能回答一句话。从那一天，也没有人敢再问他什么。  

    Jesus quotes these verses in Matthew 22:41-45 to show the Pharisees that the Messiah must be more than just a human descendant of David; He must be the pre-existent, the divine Lord. Jesus said the author of the psalm David was inspired, speaking by the Spirit, so the Holy Spirit told us what the Holy Father said to the Holy Son, that He would sit on the throne, the son of David was actually the son of God, thus confirm and affirm the deity of Christ.
    耶稣在马太福音22:41-45中引用了这些诗句，向法利赛人表明弥赛亚绝不仅是大卫的肉身存在的人类后裔，祂更是那先前已存在、神圣的主。耶稣指出，诗篇的作者大卫是受圣灵感动的，因此是圣灵告诉了我们圣父对圣子所说的话——祂要坐在宝座上。这位大卫的子孙实际上是神的儿子，从而确认并肯定了基督的神性。  

    The invitation to “sit at my right hand” (110:1) indicates both unity and distinction. The right hand signifies shared authority, yet the act of invitation maintains a distinction between the one who invites and the one being invited. The Spirit is the one revealing the eternal, heavenly conversation taking place between the Father and the Son.
    “坐在我的右边”（110:1）的邀请既表明了合一，也表明了区别。右边象征着共享的权柄，然而邀请的举动本身维持了邀请者与被邀请者之间的区别。圣灵则是将父与子之间发生的永恒、属天的对话启示出来的那一位。

Psalm 110:4 The LORD has sworn and will not change his mind: “You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek.”
诗篇 110:4 耶和华起了誓，决不后悔，说：“你是照着麦基洗德的等次永远为祭司。”  

    Hebrews 7 told us the exceptional nature of Melchizedek’s priesthood. More so, for Jesus’ priesthood as the Holy Son. 7:20 ‘Others became priests without any oath, but Jesus became a priest with an oath when God the Father said to him: The Lord the Father has sworn and will not change his mind, you the Son are a priest forever.
    希伯来书第7章告诉我们麦基洗德祭司职分的独特特殊性。对于作为圣子的耶稣的祭司职分而言更是如此。7:20提到：“再者，耶稣为祭司，并不是起誓立的；至于那些人，先前作祭司，乃是起誓立的。只有耶稣是起誓立的，因为那立他的父对他说：‘主起了誓，决不后悔，你是永远为祭司。’”——主圣父起了誓，决不后悔，你圣子永远为祭司。  

    Melchizedek, mysteriously introduced in Genesis 14 without genealogy, uniquely combined the roles of king and priest and received tithes from Abraham, the patriarch of Israel. The Messiah is superior even to Abraham, from whom Israel and the Levitical priesthood descended. God the Son has a greater authority than any of Israel’s founding ancestors.
    麦基洗德在创世记14章中被神秘地引入，没有家谱记载，他独特地兼具了君王与祭司的双重角色，并接受了以色列先祖亚伯拉罕的十分之一奉献。弥赛亚甚至比亚伯拉罕还要尊贵，而以色列和利未的祭司职分正是从亚伯拉罕而出的。子神拥有比利未的任何一位先祖都更大的权柄。

    The Messiah holds an eternal, unchanging priesthood. Because he operates outside the traditional Levitical priesthood, Unlike the Levitical order, this priesthood isn’t based on ancestral lineage but on God’s sovereign oath. This divine appointment grants the Messiah unique authority and establishes an eternal priesthood. The phrase “forever” emphasizes its perpetual nature, transcending time and eliminating the need for generational succession. The Messiah is shown to be divine, eternal —He is God the Son.
    弥赛亚拥有永恒不变的祭司职分。因为祂在传统的利未祭司体系之外供职，与利未班次不同，这一祭司职分不是基于祖先的血统，而是基于神主权的誓言。这种神的设立赋予了弥赛亚独一无二的权柄，并确立了永恒的祭司职分。“永远”一词强调了其永久的特质，超越了时间，也无需世代更替。弥赛亚被证明是神圣、永恒的——祂就是子神。  

Isaiah 48:16 “Come near me and listen to this: “From the first announcement I have not spoken in secret; at the time it happens, I am there.” And now the Sovereign LORD has sent me, endowed with his Spirit. 17 This is what the LORD says—your Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel:
以赛亚书 48:16 “你们要就近我来听这话：‘我从头就没有在隐密处说话，自从有这事，我就在那里。’ 现在，主耶和华差遣我和他的灵来。” 17 耶和华你的救赎主，以色列的圣者如此说：  

Other than Psalm 110 and many other passages, Christians over the centuries have taken also Isaiah 48 as a witness to the Trinity, as a glorious testimony of a trinity of Persons in the Godhead; Christ the Son of God is sent in human nature, gifted with the Spirit, as a proof of the mission, commission, and authority of Christ, who came not of himself, but was sent of God, (John 8:42), God the Son, is the Redeemer and Saviour; God the Spirit, is the Convincer and Comforter; (John 14:26) (15:26) (16:7,8)
除了诗篇110篇和许多其他经文外，几个世纪以来的基督徒也将以赛亚书48章视为三位一体的见证，视其为神圣位格中三位一体的荣耀彰显。神之子基督是以人性的样式被差遣，并被赐予圣灵，这证明了基督的使命、托付与权柄——祂不是凭着自己来的，乃是由神差遣的（约翰福音8:42）。子神是救赎主和救主；灵神是使人知罪自省者与保惠师（安慰者）（约翰福音14:26；15:26；16:7,8）。

“雅各，我所选召的以色列啊，当听我言：我是耶和华，我是首先的，也是末后的。”

Who else could this be? But the son Jesus Christ who proclaims and promises in Revelations 22:12 ‘Behold, I am coming soon! My reward is with me, and I will give to everyone according to what he has done. Verse 13 – I am the Alpha and the Omega, the First and the Last, the Beginning, and the End.
这还能是谁呢？除了儿子耶稣基督，祂在启示录22:12中宣告并应许道：“看哪，我必快来！赏罚在我，要照各人所行的报应他。” 第13节——“我是阿拉法，我是俄梅戛；我是首先的，我是末后的；我是初，我是终。”

After his resurrection, and before his ascension, Jesus said ‘all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.. …in the name of the triune God, in the name of the Holy Trinity, the tri unity.
耶稣在复活之后、升天之前说道：“天上地下所有的权柄都赐给我了。所以，你们要去，使万民作我的门徒，奉父、子、圣灵的名给他们施洗……” 奉三位一体神的名，奉神圣三一、三合一的名。  

Ephesians 2:18- For through the Son, we have access to the Father by the Spirit. Through the Son, by the Spirit, we obtain and achieve reconciliation. First we reconcile to God, then we reconcile to others, to enemies, to aliens, to all who are even very different from us.
以弗所书 2:18——因为我们两下借着他（子），被一个圣灵所感，得以进到父面前。借着圣子、借着圣灵，我们获得并实现了和好。首先，我们与神和好；然后，我们与他人和好、与仇敌和好、与外人以及所有甚至与我们截然不同的人和好。  

We acknowledge the Stolen Generations, we acknowledge the grieves and the pains when the land was taken, the resources abused. We pray for reconciliation between indigenous and non indigenous people, we need to be ‘all in’, because reconciliation is the gospel value, based on the Trinity concept, so we can’t be a spectator, but all to step away from the sidelines to actively and prayerfully commit to change, commit to do better.
我们承认“被偷走的一代”，我们承认土地被强占、资源被滥用时的悲伤与痛苦。我们为原住民与非原住民之间的和好祷告，我们需要“全心投入”，因为和好是基于三位一体概念的福音价值。因此我们不能做旁观者，而是要一同走出边缘，在行动和祷告中致力于改变，致力于做得更好。

May the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all and ever more Amen.
