# **Matthew 11:9–11**  
# 马太福音 11:9–11  
A prophet? Yes, I tell you, and more than a prophet.  
是先知吗？是的，我告诉你们，他比先知更大。  
This is the one about whom it is written:  
正如经上所记：  
“I will send my messenger ahead of you, who will prepare your way before you.”  
“看哪！我要差遣我的使者在你前面，预备你的道路。”  
Truly I tell you, among those born of women there has not risen anyone greater than John the Baptist;  
我实在告诉你们，凡妇人所生的，没有一个兴起来大过施洗约翰的；  
yet whoever is least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he.  
然而，在天国里最小的，比他还大。

---

In the Bible, a prophet is primarily a spokesperson chosen by God to deliver His word and will.  
在圣经中，先知主要是上帝所拣选的代言人，向人宣告祂的话语和旨意。  
John the Baptist fulfilled the Old Testament prophecy about a messenger who would “prepare the way for the Lord.”  
施洗约翰实现了旧约预言：那位在旷野中“预备主道路”的使者。

Jesus called John the greatest prophet, yet said that even the least in the Kingdom is greater than he.  
耶稣称约翰为最伟大的先知，但祂也说天国里最小的都比他大。  
This sounds contradictory, yet Jesus meant: John was great because he pointed people to the coming Kingdom,  
这似乎矛盾，但耶稣的意思是：约翰伟大，因为他指引人进入天国；  
but the disciples and all believers are greater because they actually enter and live in that Kingdom.  
但门徒和所有信徒更大，因为他们真正进入并活在天国里。  
Blessed are those who repent, confess Christ as Saviour, bear fruit for Him, and draw others into God’s joyful Kingdom.  
那些悔改、承认基督为救主、为主结果子，并吸引更多人进入神荣耀喜乐之国的人，是有福的。

# **Isaiah 35:1–10**  
# 以赛亚书 35:1–10  
1 The desert and parched land will be glad; the wilderness will rejoice and blossom.  
1 旷野和干旱之地必然欢喜，沙漠也必快乐，又像番红花一样开花。  
2 It will burst into bloom; it will rejoice greatly and shout for joy…  
2 必开花繁盛，大大欢喜，高声欢呼……  
(全文略同原文，以双语呈现)  
（以下经文按原文逐句译为中文）

3 Strengthen the feeble hands, steady the knees that give way;  
3 你们要坚固软弱的手，使无力的膝稳固；  
4 say to those with fearful hearts, “Be strong, do not fear; your God will come… He will come to save you.”  
4 对胆怯的人说：“你们要刚强，不要害怕！看哪，你们的神必来……祂必来拯救你们。”  
5 Then will the eyes of the blind be opened and the ears of the deaf unstopped.  
5 那时瞎子的眼必睁开，聋子的耳必开通；  
6 Then will the lame leap like a deer and the mute tongue shout for joy…  
6 那时瘸子必像鹿一样跳跃，哑巴的舌头必能欢呼……  
10 …Everlasting joy will crown their heads… sorrow and sighing will flee away.  
10 ……永恒的喜乐必临到他们，忧愁叹息必然逃避。

---

If we look at the world only through gloom—wars, violence, climate crisis, extinction, scarcity, lack of manpower, a church without a vicar or organist—everything seems bleak.  
若我们只从忧郁的眼光看世界——战争、暴力、气候危机、物种灭绝、资源匮乏、人手不足、没有牧师、没有司琴的教会——一切都显得阴暗。  

But let us remove the spectacles of pessimism and put on the spectacles of optimism.  
但让我们摘下悲观的眼镜，戴上乐观的眼镜。  
Let us see the world with the perspective of the Kingdom.  
让我们以天国的视角看世界。  

Isaiah shows us a world where deserts bloom, joy overflows, and God’s splendour fills the earth.  
以赛亚向我们展示一个沙漠开花、喜乐满溢、上帝荣耀遍满的世界。  
One day, this joy will characterize all of life.  
有一天，这样的喜乐将充满我们生活的每一天。  

C. S. Lewis wrote, “Joy will be the serious business of heaven.”  
C. S. 路易斯写道：“喜乐是天堂最严肃的事。”  

When God renews all things, sorrow will flee like mice chased by cats, and joy will fill the skies like a wind clearing the clouds.  
当上帝更新万物时，忧伤会像逃离猫的老鼠般消失无踪，喜乐会如清风吹散乌云后展现的蓝天般充满世界。

---

As John pointed to Jesus, we also point people toward the joyful Kingdom.  
正如施洗约翰指向耶稣，我们也指向那喜乐的天国。  
God came to us in Christ, and He will come again.  
上帝在基督里来到我们中间，祂还要再来。  
In Jesus there is healing, salvation, and redemption.  
在耶稣里有医治、有拯救、有救赎。  
His Kingdom is full of splendour and joy.  
祂的国度满有荣耀与喜乐。

## **James 5:7–10**  
## 雅各书 5:7–10  
Be patient, then, brothers and sisters, until the Lord’s coming.  
所以弟兄们，你们要忍耐，直到主来。  
See how the farmer waits for the land to yield its valuable crop…  
看农夫怎样等候地里宝贵的出产……  
You too, be patient and stand firm…  
你们也要忍耐，坚固你们的心……  
Don’t grumble against one another…  
弟兄们，不要彼此埋怨……  
Take the prophets as an example of patience in suffering.  
你们要以先知为忍受苦难、存心忍耐的榜样。

St. James teaches us to be patient, not to grumble, to stay calm and resilient.  
雅各教导我们要忍耐，不抱怨，保持冷静与坚韧。  
We can only be patient when we have hope—just like farmers who believe their crops will grow.  
我们之所以能忍耐，是因为心里有盼望——就像农夫深信庄稼必会成长。  
We wait because the Lord’s coming is real and near.  
我们忍耐，因为主的再来是真实且临近的。

The Lord stands at the door; He judges our faith and our patience.  
主已站在门前；祂察验我们的信心与忍耐。

## My friends at St David’s, no matter what hurdles we face—  
## 亲爱的圣大卫堂的朋友们，无论我们面对什么难关——  
in life or in church—without an organist or a vicar—  
无论在生活中或教会中——即使没有司琴，没有牧师——  
be strong, do not fear.  
要刚强，不要害怕。  
Our God will come to help and save us.  
我们的上帝必来帮助我们、拯救我们。  

If the lame can leap like a deer and the mute can shout for joy,  
若瘸腿的能像鹿般跳跃，哑巴能欢呼讴歌，  
then surely we, too, can survive and thrive with faith, resilience, and determination.  
那么我们也必能靠着信心、坚韧与决心，不但存活，更能兴旺。  

May God help us. Amen.  
愿上帝帮助我们。阿们。
