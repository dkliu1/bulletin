# Year A - EASTER 2 | 复活节后第二主日

Date: 12/4/2026

Scripture (和合本): * Acts / 使徒行传 2:14a, 22-32

    Psalm / 诗篇 16

    1 Peter / 彼得前书 1:1-12

    John / 约翰福音 20:19-31

The Story of Thomas | 多马的故事

On this Sunday, the second Sunday of Easter, it is our tradition to read the story of the doubts of Thomas. I suppose we can call him the Patron Saint of all those who have difficulty conceiving of and believing in the resurrection.
在这个主日，即复活节后的第二个主日，按照传统我们会读到多马怀疑的故事。我想我们可以称他为所有那些难以想象和相信复活的人的“主保圣徒”。

Yet the story of Thomas is not only a story of doubt, but a story of the way that the risen Christ ministers to doubt.
然而，多马的故事不仅是一个关于怀疑的故事，更是一个关于复活的基督如何面对和服事怀疑的故事。

From today’s gospel narrative we learn at least two core truths:
从今天的福音叙述中，我们至少学到两个核心真理：

    Doubt was present from the beginning: Doubt of the resurrection was present among the disciples from the beginning; we are not the first generation of believers to have difficulty embracing the fact of the resurrection.
    怀疑自始就有： 对复活的怀疑从一开始就存在于门徒中间；我们并不是第一代难以接受复活事实的信徒。

    Jesus reassures through His presence: Jesus reassures our doubts by his living presence. Belief in the resurrection is a gift of the risen Lord.
    耶稣以同在坚固信心： 耶稣通过祂活生生的同在来消除我们的疑虑。相信复活是复活之主赐予的礼物。

The Centrality of Resurrection | 复活的核心地位

A Christian is someone who believes that God raised Jesus from the dead. There has never been a time, when this belief has not been questioned.
基督徒就是相信上帝使耶稣从死里复活的人。这种信仰从未有过不被质疑的时候。

When the apostle Paul, in Athens, preached Jesus’ resurrection from the dead (Acts 17) he was greeted with hoots of derision. The non-Christian world still laughs at this central Christian belief. Even in this morning’s gospel, we find Jesus’ own disciples having doubts.
当使徒保罗在雅典传讲耶稣从死里复活时（使徒行传17章），他遭到了嘲笑。非基督徒世界至今仍在嘲笑这一基督教的核心信仰。甚至在今早的福音书中，我们也看到耶稣自己的门徒也在怀疑。

Thomas said he would not believe unless the risen Christ stood before him. The risen Christ did just that. Thomas believed and so must we. No historian doubts that without the belief in the resurrection, there would be no gospels, no Christians. It was the resurrection that produced it.
多马说，除非复活的基督站在他面前，否则他不信。复活的基督正是这样做的。多马信了，我们也必须信。没有哪位历史学家会怀疑：如果没有对复活的信仰，就不会有福音书，也不会有基督徒。是复活创造了这一切。
Overcoming Prejudice | 克服先入为主的偏见

Yet the story of doubting Thomas is a reminder of the incredible absurdity of such a thing happening in time and space. First-century people may not have been scientists, but they all knew that dead men don’t rise to new life.
然而，多马怀疑的故事提醒我们，在时空之中发生这样的事情是多么不可思议。一世纪的人可能不是科学家，但他们都知道死人不会复生。

In every age, belief in the resurrection of Jesus must overcome a strong prior prejudice against the possibility of such a thing happening, because it runs counter to our expectation, based on every day experience.
在每一个时代，对耶稣复活的信仰都必须克服一种强烈的先入为主的偏见，即这种事不可能发生，因为它违背了我们基于日常经验的预期。

The resurrection is a jolt to anybody’s imagination, even if they are not "doubting Thomases":
复活对任何人的想象力都是一种震撼，即使他们不是“多马”：

    “她们就出来，从坟墓那里逃跑，又发抖又惊奇，什么也不告诉人，因为她们害怕。” (马可福音 16:8)
    "They went out and fled from the tomb; for trembling and astonishment had come upon them; and they said nothing to anyone, for they were afraid." (Mark 16:8)

The Nature of Resurrection | 复活的性质

Here we must assert a few central - core - absolutely trustworthy facts about the resurrection. This is our Easter Faith: we believe, against our natural tendencies to disbelieve, in the resurrection of Jesus as a "bodily resurrection".
在此，我们必须断言关于复活的几个核心且绝对可信的事实。这就是我们的复活节信仰：尽管我们天生倾向于不信，但我们相信耶稣的复活是“身体的复活”。

In the Apostles' Creed, we confess that we believe in "the resurrection of the body." The ground of this confession is the prior belief in the bodily resurrection of Jesus. We hold this belief against two common tendencies:
在《使徒信经》中，我们承认相信“身体复活”。这一承认的基础是首先相信耶稣身体的复活。我们持守这一信念，以对抗两种常见的倾向：

    Reduction to resuscitation: To reduce it to a reviving of a body in an ICU.
    简化为复苏： 将其简化为在重症监护室（ICU）里的起死回生。

    Spiritualization: To "spiritualize" the event, as though his spirit or soul was raised like a ghost, leaving his body (or "physical container") behind.
    属灵化： 将这一事件“属灵化”，仿佛祂的灵魂像鬼魂一样升起，而把身体（或称“物理容器”）留在身后。

For Paul, the resurrection meant "new life" in a "new body" — not the return of the same old life in a perishable physical body. It is Jesus’ earthly mortal body that is transformed into an immortal spiritual body.
对于保罗来说，复活意味着在“新身体”中的“新生命”——而不是旧生命回到必朽坏的肉身中。是耶稣属地的必死身体转化为不朽的属灵身体。
The Foundation of Faith | 信仰的根基

It is not an exaggeration to say that the entire structure of the Christian faith stands or falls upon the fact of Jesus being raised by God from the dead.
可以毫不夸张地说，整个基督教信仰的架构都取决于上帝使耶稣从死里复活这一事实。

Who is God? What is God up to in the world? Who are we as children of God? What are we supposed to be up to? All these questions are answered through the resurrection.
上帝是谁？上帝在世界上做什么？作为上帝的孩子，我们是谁？我们应该做什么？所有这些问题都通过复活得到了解答。

    “上帝就是那叫基督从死里复活的。” (参 罗马书 11:15/4:24)
    God is the one who "raised Christ from the dead." (cf. Romans 11:15)

Likewise, the resurrection says who we are. This world, for all its goodness, is not the end, not the ultimate destination of human life.
同样地，复活定义了我们是谁。这个世界尽管有其美好之处，但并不是人类生命的终点或终极目的地。

    “愿颂赞归与我们主耶稣基督的父神！他曾照自己的大怜悯，藉耶稣基督从死里复活，重生了我们，叫我们有活泼的盼望。” (彼得前书 1:3)
    "By his great mercy God has given us a new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead." (1 Peter 1:3).

Mission and Distinctiveness | 使命与独特

The resurrection is also a call to mission. The risen Christ tells his followers to go and tell the whole world that Jesus Christ is both crucified and risen.
复活也是一种使命的召唤。复活的基督告诉祂的追随者，去告诉全世界：耶稣基督既被钉十字架，也复活了。

At the present, there is uncertainty among some Christians whether it is important to preach the Gospel to people of other faiths. Do not all religions say the same thing, only in different ways?
目前，一些基督徒对于向其他信仰的人传福音是否重要感到迟疑。难道不是所有的宗教都在以不同的方式说同样的事情吗？

As far as I am concerned, Christianity is not the same as other religions. To say it is is patent nonsense. While I have seen many Christians express cynicism and doubt - I have never encountered a Hindu, Muslim or Sikh convert to Christianity lightly and turn his back on his Christian faith.
就我而言，基督教与其他宗教并不相同。说它们相同纯属胡言。虽然我见过许多基督徒表达愤世嫉俗和怀疑，但我从未遇到过一个印度教徒、穆斯林或锡克教徒在皈依基督教后会轻易地背弃他的基督信仰。
A Personal Testimony: Sharma | 个人见证：夏尔马

I personally knew one such man, Rajendra Kumar Sharma, an Orthodox Hindu, Brahmin — a self-made man. He became a Christian after being invited to St John’s church Youth Fellowship. I witnessed his baptism where we sang “I have decided to follow Jesus, no turning back.”
我个人认识这样一个人，拉金德拉·库马尔·夏尔马（Rajendra Kumar Sharma），一个正统的印度教婆罗门，一个白手起家的人。他在受邀参加圣约翰教堂青年团契后成为了基督徒。我见证了他的洗礼，当时我们唱着：“我已经决定要跟随耶稣，永不回头。”

Sharma’s mother and brother rejected him, but he never rejected them. Later, he died of cancer in Melbourne in 1985 at age 37, strong in faith.
夏尔马的母亲和哥哥抛弃了他，但他从未放弃他们。后来，他于1985年在墨尔本死于癌症，年仅37岁，去世时信仰坚定。

    Hinduism says: “This is your Karma... your reward or punishment for deeds in a past life.”
    印度教说： “这是你的业力……是你前世行为的奖赏或惩罚。”

    Christianity teaches: “Christ Jesus has set you free — he has taken your Karma upon himself... you have been set free.”
    基督教教导： “基督耶稣已释放了你——祂将你的业力担在自己身上……你已获得自由。”

The Power of Transformation | 改变的力量

We believe God raised Jesus — God brings life where death reigns, he sets the prisoner free, he opens the eyes of the blind.
我们相信上帝使耶稣复活——上帝在死亡作王的地方带来生命，祂释放被囚的，开瞎子的眼。

Consider Gladys Staines, the widow of Australian missionary Graeme Staines, who was killed by extremists in 1999. She continued to serve lepers and those Hinduism wouldn’t touch. It is patent nonsense to say faith in Christ is the same as any other religion.
想想格拉迪斯·斯泰因斯（Gladys Staines），澳大利亚传教士格雷厄姆·斯泰因斯的遗孀。她的丈夫和两个儿子在1999年被极端分子杀害，但她继续服事麻风病人——那些印度教不愿碰触的人。说对基督的信仰与其他宗教一样，纯属胡说八道。

There is an unbridgeable gulf between God’s self-revelation in Christ Jesus and man’s speculation. Christ is the fulfilment of all the hopes and longings of all religions.
在上帝于基督耶稣里的自我启示与人类的推测之间，有着不可逾越的鸿沟。基督是所有宗教中一切希望和渴望的成全。
Final Charge | 最后的嘱托

The Church has a unique truth to preach: that Jesus’ death and resurrection is God’s way of reconciling the world to himself. Without the resurrection, we are without hope. With the resurrection, we know the end of the story.
教会有一个独特的真理要传讲：耶稣的死而复活是上帝使世人与自己和好的方式。没有复活，我们就没有希望。有了复活，我们便知道故事的结局。

Doubting Thomas went to India, preached the gospel, and was martyred in Chennai. The San Thome Cathedral stands there today.
“怀疑的多马”去了印度传福音，并在金奈殉道。今天圣多马大教堂就屹立在那里。

    耶稣对他说：“你因看见了我才信；那没有看见就信的有福了。” (约翰福音 20:29)
    Jesus said to him, “Have you believed because you have seen me? Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have come to believe.”

The Lord is risen! He is risen indeed.
主复活了！祂果然复活了。
