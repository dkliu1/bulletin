# 约翰福音 9:1-41 / John 9:1-41

耶稣过去的时候，看见一个人生来是瞎眼的。门徒问耶稣说：“拉比，这人生来是瞎眼，是谁犯了罪？是这人呢？是他父母呢？”
As he went along, he saw a man blind from birth. His disciples asked him, “Rabbi, who sinned, this man or his parents, that he was born blind?”

耶稣回答说：“也不是这人犯了罪，也不是他父母犯了罪，是要在他身上显出神的作为来。趁着白日，我们必须做那差我来者的工；黑夜将到，就没有人能做工了。我在世上的时候，是世上的光。”
“Neither this man nor his parents sinned,” said Jesus, “but this happened so that the works of God might be displayed in him. As long as it is day, we must do the works of him who sent me. Night is coming, when no one can work. While I am in the world, I am the light of the world.”

耶稣说了这话，就吐唾沫在地上，用唾沫和泥抹在瞎子的眼睛上，对他说：“你往西罗亚池子里去洗”（西罗亚翻出来就是“奉差遣”）。他去一洗，回头就看见了。
After saying this, he spit on the ground, made some mud with the saliva, and put it on the man’s eyes. “Go,” he told him, “wash in the Pool of Siloam” (this word means “Sent”). So the man went and washed, and came home seeing.

他的邻舍和那素来见他是讨饭的，就说：“这不是那从前坐着讨饭的人吗？”有人说：“是他”；又有人说：“不是，却是像他。”他自己说：“是我。”
His neighbors and those who had formerly seen him begging asked, “Isn’t this the same man who used to sit and beg?” Some claimed that he was. Others said, “No, he only looks like him.” But he himself insisted, “I am the man.”

他们对他说：“你的眼睛是怎么睁开的呢？”他回答说：“有一个人，名叫耶稣，他和泥抹我的眼睛，对我说：‘你往西罗亚池子去洗。’我去一洗，就看见了。”
“How then were your eyes opened?” they asked. He replied, “The man they call Jesus made some mud and put it on my eyes. He told me to go to Siloam and wash. So I went and washed, and then I could see.”

他们问他说：“那个人在哪里？”他说：“我不知道。”他们把从前瞎眼的人带到法利赛人那里。耶稣和泥开他眼睛的日子是安息日。
“Where is this man?” they asked him. “I don’t know,” he said. They brought to the Pharisees the man who had been blind. Now the day on which Jesus had made the mud and opened the man’s eyes was a Sabbath.

法利赛人也问他是怎么得看见的。瞎子对他们说：“他把泥抹在我的眼睛上，我一洗，就看见了。”法利赛人中有的说：“这个人不是从神来的，因为他不守安息日。”又有人说：“一个罪人怎能行这样的神迹呢？”他们就起了纷争。
Therefore the Pharisees also asked him how he had received his sight. “He put mud on my eyes,” the man replied, “and I washed, and now I see.” Some of the Pharisees said, “This man is not from God, for he does not keep the Sabbath.” But others asked, “How can a sinner perform such signs?” So they were divided.

他们又对瞎子说：“他既然开了你的眼睛，你说他是怎样的人呢？”他说：“是个先知。”
Then they turned again to the blind man, “What have you to say about him? It was your eyes he opened.” The man replied, “He is a prophet.”

犹太人不信他从前是瞎眼，后来能看见的，等到叫了他的父母来，问他们说：“这是你们的儿子吗？你们说他生来是瞎眼的，现在怎么能看见了呢？”
They still did not believe that he had been blind and had received his sight until they sent for the man’s parents. “Is this your son?” they asked. “Is this the one you say was born blind? How is it that now he can see?”

他父母回答说：“他是我们的儿子，生来是瞎眼的，这是我们知道的。至于他现在怎么能看见，我们不知道；是谁开了他的眼睛，我们也不知道。他已经成年，你们问他吧，他自己必能说。”
“We know he is our son,” the parents answered, “and we know he was born blind. But how he can see now, or who opened his eyes, we don’t know. Ask him. He is of age; he will speak for himself.”

他父母说这话，是怕犹太人；因为犹太人已经商议定了，若有认耶稣是基督的，要把他赶出会堂。因此他父母说：“他已经成年，你们问他吧。”
His parents said this because they were afraid of the Jewish leaders, who already had decided that anyone who acknowledged that Jesus was the Messiah would be put out of the synagogue. That was why his parents said, “He is of age; ask him.”

所以法利赛人第二次叫了那从前瞎眼的人来，对他说：“你该将荣耀归给神，我们知道这人是个罪人。”
A second time they summoned the man who had been blind. “Give glory to God by telling the truth,” they said. “We know this man is a sinner.”

那人回答说：“他是个罪人不是，我不知道；有一件事我知道，从前我是瞎眼的，如今能看见了。”
He replied, “Whether he is a sinner or not, I don’t know. One thing I do know: I was blind but now I see!”

耶稣听说他们把他赶出去，后来遇见他，就说：“你信神的儿子吗？”他回答说：“主啊，谁是神的儿子，叫我信他呢？”
Jesus heard that they had thrown him out, and when he found him, he said, “Do you believe in the Son of Man?” “Who is he, sir?” the man asked. “Tell me so that I may believe in him.”

耶稣说：“你已经看见他，现在和你说话的就是他。”他说：“主啊，我信！”就拜耶稣。
Jesus said, “You have now seen him; in fact, he is the one speaking with you.” Then the man said, “Lord, I believe,” and he worshiped him.

耶稣说：“我为审判到这世上来，叫不能看见的，可以看见；能看见的，反瞎了眼。”
Jesus said, “For judgment I have come into this world, so that the blind will see and those who see will become blind.”

同他在那里的法利赛人听见这话，就说：“难道我们也瞎了眼吗？”
Some Pharisees who were with him heard him say this and asked, “What? Are we blind too?”

耶稣对他们说：“你们若瞎了眼，就没有罪了；但如今你们说‘我们能看见’，所以你们的罪还在。”
Jesus said, “If you were blind, you would not be guilty of sin; but now that you claim you can see, your guilt remains.”
## 默想与信息 / Reflection and Message

在预苦期中，我们哀悼耶稣受的苦难。他本无罪，却在痛苦、折磨、羞辱中死去，并与他所爱的人、与他的天父分离。
During Lent, we lament Jesus’ sufferings. One who was innocent but died in agony, in pain, in humiliation, and in separation with his loved ones, with his heavenly Father.

在2026年3月的预苦期里——我们不知道这种情况还要持续多久——自2月28日以来，我们哀悼许多在战争中死去的无辜生命。当伊朗爆发战争，以色列和美国发动联合空袭，作为回击与报复，导弹和无人机齐发，夺走了数千人的生命。随着战争升级，波及的国家越来越多，更多的平民、士兵和无辜者正在死去……
During Lent 2026, in March and we don’t know how much longer, since 28 February, we lament the many innocent people died and have been dying when wars began in Iran, when Israel and the United States launched joint air attacks, in return, and in retaliation, missiles and drones were fired, killing thousands, affecting and involving more and more countries, as the wars escalated, more civilians, soldiers and innocent people died and are dying…

现在中东和欧洲各地的机场陷入混乱，航班取消，石油价格飙升，供应短缺……我们难道不该为人类罪恶的沉重、战争罪行的严酷、人的骄傲与偏见、以及人的瞎眼而哀恸吗？人们看不见神的愤怒、神的审判，也听不见神因人的哀号与流血而发出的叹息。
Now the airports around the Middle East and Europe are in chaos, flights are cancelled, petrol price soar as petrol supply is running low… can we not lament the gravity of human sins, the severity of war crimes, the pride and prejudice, and the blindness of people, who do not see God’s anger, God’s judgement and God’s disappointment hearing people’s cries, seeing people’s blood shed.

自世界起首以来，人的罪恶就带来了苦难。当我们违反世俗法律时，会遭受后果；而当我们违背上帝的神圣法则时，更是如此。在约翰福音第5章中，耶稣医治了一个瘫痪38年的人，耶稣对他说：“你看，你已经痊愈了，不要再犯罪，恐怕你遭遇的更加利害。”
Since the beginning of the world, human sins bring along human sufferings. We suffer from the consequences when we break any civil laws, more so, when we break God’s divine laws. In John 5 when Jesus healed a man who has been crippled for 38 years, Jesus said to him, ‘See, you are well again. Stop sinning or something worse may happen to you.’

我们在某种程度上认同“因果报应”的基本原则，例如在中国或印度的宗教（如印度教或佛教）中，他们相信业力：如果你现在做了坏事，以后就会有不幸降临；如果你行善，就会有好报。
We agree with some degrees the fundamental principle of cause and effect, sometimes in some Chinese or Indian religions: Hinduism or Buddhism for example, they believe in karma: If you do something bad now, something bad will happen to you later. But if you do something nice or good for someone else and then something good will happen to you.

作为基督徒，圣经告诉我们，对上帝缺乏信心会导致我们犯罪，但对耶稣的信心能救赎我们的罪。在马可福音第2章，当耶稣见他们的信心，就对瘫子说：“小子，你的罪赦了。”
As Christians, the Bible says lacking faith in God causes us to sin, but faith in Jesus redeems our sins. In Mark 2, When Jesus saw their faith, he said to the paralytic, “Son, your sins are forgiven.”

在今天的福音读经中，耶稣启发了我们关于人类苦难的另一个原因。耶稣说：“也不是这人犯了罪，也不是他父母犯了罪……（导致他生来瞎眼）”，“是要在他身上显出神的作为来。”疾病、残疾、癌症、灾难、危机甚至战争发生了……是为了让神的作为在其中彰显。
In today’s Gospel reading, Jesus inspires us with another reason of human sufferings. Jesus said “Neither this man nor his parents sinned… which caused a man born blind.” “but this happened.. illness, disability, cancer, disasters, catastrophes, crises, even wars happened… so that the works of God might be displayed in it…”

神的作为如此彰显：首先，这人无缘无故生来瞎眼；第二，耶稣有目的地遇见了他；第三，耶稣运用神圣的能力医治了他，展现了神的主权。耶稣吐唾沫在地上，用泥抹在瞎子的眼睛上，对他说：“你往西罗亚池子里去洗。”他去一洗，回头就看见了。
The works of God displayed, first the man was born blind… out of no reason… second Jesus met this man for a reason… third, Jesus healed this man using his divine power, presenting his divine sovereignty, as Jesus spit on the ground, made some mud with the saliva, and put it on the blind man’s eyes. “Go,” he told him, “wash in the Pool of Siloam” (this word means “Sent”). So the man went and washed, and came home seeing.

为了能看见神的作为彰显，我们需要信靠与顺服，需要有信心，需要去相信。开启我们的双眼，我们或许能看见医治、神迹、冲突的终结、战争的平息以及所有苦难的消散……因为耶稣已经代表我们死在十字架上，为了我们的益处。耶稣修复了我们的关系，恢复了我们丧失的良知，使我们与作为天父的神和好。
To be able to see God’s works display, we need to trust and obey, we need to have faith and we need to believe. Open our eyes, we may see healings, miracles, end of conflicts, end of wars, end of all sufferings… as Jesus has suffered on the cross on our behalf, for our benefits. Jesus repaired our relationship, restored our lost conscience, reconciled us to God as our Heavenly Father.

上周我们听到耶稣如何服事撒玛利亚妇人，使她信服、改变，并受差遣去作见证，帮助许多人也看见耶稣就是基督和弥赛亚。
Last week we heard how Jesus ministered to the Samaritan woman so she was convinced, she was converted, she was sent to testify and helped many to see also Jesus as Christ and Messiah.

今天我们看到这个瞎子被医治，他通过向邻舍和法利赛人述说真相来归荣耀给神。那真相是什么？真相就是这瞎子反复述说的：“那个人名叫耶稣，他和泥抹我的眼睛，对我说：‘你往西罗亚池子去洗。’我去一洗，就看见了。”
Today we see this blind man was healed, and he gave glory to God by telling the truth to his neighbours then to the Pharisees, what was the truth.. the truth the blind man said it repeatedly: “… The man they call Jesus made some mud and put it on my eyes. He told me to go to Siloam and wash. So I went and washed, and then I could see.”

述说真相可能荣耀神，但也可能给我们带来麻烦。这瞎子仅仅是谈论耶稣，就被强行赶出了会堂。耶稣听说他被赶出来，遇见他时问：“你信神的儿子吗？”那人说：“主啊，我信！”就拜耶稣。
Speaking the truth may glorify God but also may cause us troubles. The blind man simply talked about Jesus but was put out of the synagogue by force. Jesus heard that they had thrown him out, and when he found him, he said, “Do you believe in the Son of Man?” Then the man said, “Lord, I believe,” and he worshiped Jesus.

神的作为有时彰显在奇迹中，有时彰显在软弱、悲剧甚至迫害中……当我们困苦、挫折和失望时亲近神，我们就能经历神、看见神、寻见神。我们的信心会增长，我们的信仰会变得真实。我们坦诚地讲述自己的故事，预备好分享见证，忍不住要敬拜神，满心欢喜地服事耶稣。
God’s work displayed… sometimes in wonders, sometimes in infirmities, in tragedies, or even in persecutions…… when we come close to God in times of troubles, in times of frustrations and disappointments, we experience God, we see God, we find God. Our faith increase, our belief become authentic. We are honest to tell our stories, we are ready to share our testimonies, we can’t stop to worship God, we are so happy to serve Jesus.

希腊语中的“敬拜”（proskuneō）也可以译为“跪下”或“俯伏在地”，意指对神极其真诚的敬畏、深切的感激以及谦卑的顺服。因为神伟大而怜悯的作为，通过为我们众人受苦受难的儿子耶稣彰显了出来。
The Greek word for the act of worship (proskuneō) could also be translated “to kneel” or “to prostrate oneself on the ground,” meaning the very sincere reverence, the very respectful gratitude, the very humble submission to God whose great and merciful works displayed, through his Son Jesus who died and suffered for us all.

18世纪英国长老会牧师和评论家马修·亨利（Matthew Henry）曾说：“……苦难和艰辛有时纯粹是为了上帝的荣耀和彰显祂的作为。上帝对祂所有的受造物拥有主权和排他性的权利，祂可以按照祂认为合适的方式，或通过作为，或通过受苦，使受造物为祂的荣耀服务；如果上帝通过我们或在我们身上得到荣耀，我们就不是虚度此生。”
Matthew Henry, a British Presbyterian minister and commentator in the 18th century once said, “...afflictions and hardship are sometimes intended purely for the glory of God, and the manifesting of his works. God has a sovereignty over all his creatures and an exclusive right in them, and may make them serviceable to his glory in such a way as he thinks fit, in doing or suffering; and if God be glorified, either by us or in us, we were not made in vain.”

如果我们用属灵的眼光看见神作为的彰显，我们的受苦就不会徒然。当我们看到上帝如何安慰与医治，如何介入以带来公义与和平时，战争也就不会徒然发生。
We do not suffer in vain, if we see, with our spiritual eyes the work of God displays. The wars won’t happen in vain, when we see how God may comfort and heal, how God may intervene to bring justice and peace.

耶稣绝对没有白白死去，因为他战胜了罪恶与死亡，摧毁了撒旦及其黑暗政权，为我们通往安全幸福的生活开辟了新路。
Definitely and certainly, Jesus did not die in vain, when he defeated sins and deaths, when he destroyed Satan and its dark regime, when he opened up new ways for our safe and happy life.

让我们祷告：感谢耶稣告诉我们真相——这人生来瞎眼并非出于惩罚或折磨，不是因为他在母腹中犯了罪，也不是因为他父母是罪人。人们遭受苦难和无辜死去绝非你的心意。请帮助我们看明战争与灾难的目的与意义。请帮助我们继续宣认你，并敬拜你这位荣耀的受难基督。阿门。
Let us pray: Thank you Jesus for telling us the truth that the man wasn’t born blind as a form of punishment or torment. He wasn’t born blind because he sinned in his mother’s womb or because his parents were sinners. It is not your preference that people suffer and die innocently. Please help us to see the purpose and the meaning of wars and disasters. Please help us to keep professing you and worshipping you as the glorious Christ crucified. Amen.
