# Scripture: John 4:5-42

于是到了撒马利亚的一座城，名叫叙加，靠近雅各给他儿子约瑟的那块地。在那里有雅各井；耶稣因走路困乏，就坐在井旁。那时约有午正。
So he came to a town in Samaria called Sychar, near the plot of ground Jacob had given to his son Joseph. Jacob’s well was there, and Jesus, tired as he was from the journey, sat down by the well. It was about noon.

有一个撒马利亚的妇人来打水。耶稣对她说：“请你给我水喝。”（那时门徒进城买食物去了。）
When a Samaritan woman came to draw water, Jesus said to her, “Will you give me a drink?” (His disciples had gone into the town to buy food.)

撒马利亚的妇人对他说：“你既是犹太人，怎么向我一个撒马利亚妇人要水喝呢？”（原来犹太人和撒马利亚人没有来往。）
The Samaritan woman said to him, “You are a Jew and I am a Samaritan woman. How can you ask me for a drink?” (For Jews do not associate with Samaritans.)

耶稣回答说：“你若知道神的恩赐，和对你说‘给我水喝’的是谁，你必早求他，他也必早给了你活水。”
Jesus answered her, “If you knew the gift of God and who it is that asks you for a drink, you would have asked him and he would have given you living water.”

妇人说：“先生，没有打水的器具，井又深，从哪里得活水呢？我们的祖宗雅各将这井留给我们，他自己和儿子并牲畜也都喝这井里的水，难道你比他还大吗？”
“Sir,” the woman said, “you have nothing to draw with and the well is deep. Where can you get this living water? Are you greater than our father Jacob, who gave us the well and drank from it himself, as did also his sons and his livestock?”

耶稣回答说：“凡喝这水的还要再渴；人若喝我所赐的水就永远不渴。我所赐的水要在他里头成为泉源，直涌到永生。”
Jesus answered, “Everyone who drinks this water will be thirsty again, but whoever drinks the water I give them will never thirst. Indeed, the water I give them will become in them a spring of water welling up to eternal life.”

妇人说：“先生，请把这水赐给我，叫我不渴，也不用来这么远打水。”
The woman said to him, “Sir, give me this water so that I won’t get thirsty and have to keep coming here to draw water.”

耶稣说：“你去打发你的丈夫也到这里来。”妇人说：“我没有丈夫。”
He told her, “Go, call your husband and come back.” “I have no husband,” she replied.

耶稣说：“你说没有丈夫是不错的。你已经有五个丈夫，你现在有的并不是你的丈夫。你这话是真的。”
Jesus said to her, “You are right when you say you have no husband. The fact is, you have had five husbands, and the man you now have is not your husband. What you have just said is quite true.”

妇人说：“先生，我看出你是先知。我们的祖宗在这山上礼拜，你们倒说，应当礼拜的地方是在耶路撒冷。”
“Sir,” the woman said, “I can see that you are a prophet. Our ancestors worshiped on this mountain, but you Jews claim that the place where we must worship is in Jerusalem.”

耶稣说：“妇人，你当信我。时候将到，你们拜父，也不在这山上，也不在耶路撒冷。你们所拜的，你们不知道；我们所拜的，我们知道，因为救恩是从犹太人出来的。
“Woman,” Jesus replied, “believe me, a time is coming when you will worship the Father neither on this mountain nor in Jerusalem. You Samaritans worship what you do not know; we worship what we do know, for salvation is from the Jews.

时候将到，如今就是了，那真正拜父的，要用心灵和诚实拜他，因为父要这样的人拜他。”神是个灵，所以拜他的必须用心灵和诚实拜他。”
Yet a time is coming and has now come when the true worshipers will worship the Father in the Spirit and in truth, for they are the kind of worshipers the Father seeks. God is spirit, and his worshipers must worship in the Spirit and in truth.”

妇人说：“我知道弥赛亚（就是那称为基督的）要来；他来了，必将一切的事都告诉我们。”
The woman said, “I know that Messiah” (called Christ) “is coming. When he comes, he will explain everything to us.”

耶稣说：“这和你说话的就是他！”当下门徒回来，就希奇耶稣和一个妇人说话；只是没有人说：“你是要什么？”或说：“你为什么和她说话？”
Then Jesus declared, “I, the one speaking to you—I am he.” Just then his disciples returned and were surprised to find him talking with a woman. But no one asked, “What do you want?” or “Why are you talking with her?”

那妇人就留下水罐子，往城里去，对众人说：“你们来看！有一个人将我素来所行的一切事都给我说出来了，莫非这就是基督吗？”
Then, leaving her water jar, the woman went back to the town and said to the people, “Come, see a man who told me everything I ever did. Could this be the Messiah?”

众人就出城，往耶稣那里去。这其间，门徒对耶稣说：“拉比，请吃。”
They came out of the town and made their way toward him. Meanwhile his disciples urged him, “Rabbi, eat something.”

耶稣说：“我有食物吃，是你们不知道的。”门徒就彼此对问说：“莫非有人拿什么给他吃吗？”
But he said to them, “I have food to eat that you know nothing about.” Then his disciples said to each other, “Could someone have brought him food?”

耶稣说：“我的食物就是遵行差我来者的旨意，作成他的工。
“My food,” said Jesus, “is to do the will of him who sent me and to finish his work.

你们岂不回答说‘到收割的时候还有四个月’吗？我告诉你们，举目向田观看，庄稼已经熟了，可以收割了。
Don’t you have a saying, ‘It’s still four months until harvest’? I tell you, open your eyes and look at the fields! They are ripe for harvest.

收割的人得工价，积蓄五谷到永生，叫撒种的和收割的一同快乐。俗语说‘那人撒种，这人收割’，这话可见是真的。
Even now the one who reaps draws a wage and harvests a crop for eternal life, so that the sower and the reaper may be glad together. Thus the saying ‘One sows and another reaps’ is true.

我差你们去收你们所没有劳苦的；别人劳苦，你们享受他们所劳苦的。”
I sent you to reap what you have not worked for. Others have done the hard work, and you have reaped the benefits of their labor.”

那城里有许多撒马利亚人信了耶稣，因为那妇人作见证说：“他将我素来所行的一切事都给我说出来了。”
Many of the Samaritans from that town believed in him because of the woman’s testimony, “He told me everything I ever did.”

于是撒马利亚人来见耶稣，求他在他们那里住下，他便在那里住了两天。因耶稣的话，信的人就更多了，
So when the Samaritans came to him, they urged him to stay with them, and he stayed two days. And because of his words many more became believers.

便对妇人说：“现在我们信，不是因为你的话，是我们亲自听见了，知道这便真是救世主。”
They said to the woman, “We no longer believe just because of what you said; now we have heard for ourselves, and we know that this man really is the Savior of the world.”
## 讲道内容
Sermon Content

在今天国际妇女节之际，我愿向此时此刻坐在这里的所有女士和女性致敬……我为你们作为女性的生命、本性、性格和优点，为你们作为基督里的姐妹在圣大卫教堂的贡献与服事，为你们作为优雅淑女的美丽与高贵，向神和造物主致谢。
I would love to pay tributes today, on International Women’s day, to all ladies and women sitting here… I thank God and the Creator for your being, your nature, character and merits as a female, your contributions and services at St David’s as a sister in Christ, your beauty and your elegance as a graceful fair lady.

我也向在事工中服事的女性，以及所有从事女性事工的人致敬。女性事工意味着女性通过任何项目、活动、组织或教会，接受基督的事奉。
I pay tributes also to the women in ministry, and to all who are doing women ministry. Women ministry means women who are receiving Christ’s ministry, through any programs, activities, from any organizations or churches.

我们今天从长篇福音读经中听到的那位撒马利亚妇人，是女性事工的一个经典范例——不是通过基督的任何使者，而是直接通过基督本人。
The Samaritan woman we hear from the long Gospel reading today, is a classic example of women ministry, not through any of Christ’s ambassadors, but through Christ himself directly.

当耶稣需要从犹太前往加利利时，他刻意且带着爱心地进入撒马利亚，计划在那里会见这位有罪的、不道德的、不诚实的女人。她曾撒谎欺骗说自己没有丈夫，但事实上她有过五个丈夫，或者五个事实上的丈夫，也许是从其他女人那里抢来的。
When Jesus needed to travel from Judea to Galilee, Jesus deliberately and lovingly went into Samaria, where he planned to meet with this sinful woman, this immoral woman, this dishonest woman, who lied and cheated she had no husband but in fact she had 5 husbands, or 5 de facto husbands, maybe stolen from other women.

正常的犹太男子厌恶并怀疑任何撒马利亚人，更不用说这样一个名声败坏的女人。耶稣时代的多数犹太人蔑视撒马利亚人，甚至比讨厌外邦人更甚，因为撒马利亚人是“混血儿”。
Normal Jewish men dislike and distrust any Samaritans, not to say such a woman with such a bad standing. Most of the Jews in Jesus’ time despised the Samaritans, disliking them even more than they dislike the gentiles, because the Samaritans are ‘half-breeds’.

虽然现今我们仰慕并喜爱混血，但那些只信仰纯正犹太血统、心胸狭隘的犹太人，绝不会认可犹太人与非犹太人之间的通婚及其后代，尤其是犹太人与他们的仇敌巴比伦人（后来演变为撒马利亚人）之间的结合。
Or mixed breeds, whom nowadays we admire and adore, but the narrow minded Jews who believe only in pure Jewish blood would not approve intermarriages and offsprings from intermarriages between the Jews and non Jews particularly between the Jews and their enemies Babylonians who became later these Samaritans.

正常的犹太男子不会在公共场合与女性交谈，特别是像耶稣这样的拉比。他们说：“拉比严禁在公共场合向女性致意。”拉比甚至可能不在公共场合与自己的妻子、女儿或姐妹说话。
Normal Jewish men would not speak to a woman in public. Especially for a rabbi like Jesus. They say ‘a Rabbi is forbidden to greet a woman in public. A Rabbi might not even speak to his own wife or daughter or sister in public.

当时有些法利赛人被称为“淤青流血的法利赛人”，为什么？因为他们在街上看到女人时会闭上眼睛，结果闭着眼走路就撞到了墙壁和房屋，导致全身淤青流血……仅仅是为了避免在街上向女性打招呼！
There were Pharisees who were called ‘the bruised and bleeding Pharisees’ why? because they shut their eyes when they saw a woman on the street and so they walked and bumped into walls and houses while walking with their eyes closed, therefore causing the bruises and bleeding all over their body… just to avoid greeting any women on the street!”

任何人都可以是心胸狭窄、卑鄙或愚蠢的，但我们的主耶稣基督绝非如此。任何人都可以是性别歧视者、种族主义者、带有偏见、主观审判、不公平且不讲理的，但我们的救主耶稣只会充满怜悯与同情。
Anybody can be narrow minded, mean or silly and stupid, but not our Lord Jesus Christ. Anybody can be sexist, racist, discriminative, judgemental, unfair and unreasonable, but not our Saviour Jesus who would only be merciful, compassionate.

他视所有人——男女老少——在地位上都是平等的，无论其宗教和种族背景如何。然而，我们的主热切地向每个人揭示真理，热切地引导我们所有人归向神，归向他荣耀神圣的国度。
And he see all human beings: men and women, old and young as equal regardless of their religious and racial background. Yet our Lord is keen to bring forth the truth to everybody, our Lord is keen to point all of us to God, and to his glorious and holy Kingdom.

耶稣以一种如此温柔、不具侵略性的方式行事，以至于撒马利亚妇人虽然有罪且感到羞愧，却没有感到被耶稣攻击或羞辱。
Jesus did it in such a gentle and non intrusive way that the Samaritan woman though sinful and ashamed, but she didn’t feel attacked or humiliated by Jesus.

基督式的布道绝非冒犯或激进，而是具有说服力并令人印象深刻。切中要害后，撒马利亚妇人承认了耶稣，她说：“先生，我看出你是先知，你来自神，因为你展现了神的爱并述说了神的真理……”
A Christ like evangelism is not anything offensive or aggressive, but persuasive and impressive, to the point, the Samaritan woman acknowledge Jesus and she said; Sir,”, “I can see that you are a prophet, you are from God as you demonstrate God’s love and you speak God’s truth…

妇人说：“我知道弥赛亚（就是那称为基督的）要来；他来了，必将一切的事都告诉我们。”耶稣说：“这和你说话的就是他！”
25 The woman said, “I know that Messiah” (called Christ) “is coming. When he comes, he will explain everything to us.” 26 Then Jesus declared, “I, the one speaking to you—I am he.”

意识到耶稣就是弥赛亚，知道自己的罪已获赦免，这女人不再羞怯，不再向公众躲藏。她走向人群，讲述自己的故事，自然而然地成为了一名传道者……那城里有许多撒马利亚人信了耶稣，因为那妇人作见证。
Realizing Jesus is the Messiah, knowing that her sins is being forgiven, the woman is not shy anymore, she doesn’t hide herself from the public anymore, she goes to the crowds, she tells her story, she easily and naturally becomes an evangelist…. 39 Many of the Samaritans from that town believed in him because of the woman’s testimony…

司布真曾说：“基督有不同的门进入不同人的灵魂。对于一些人，他通过理智进入；对于许多人，他通过情感进入。对一些人，他经由恐惧而来；对另一些人，则经由希望；而对这个女人，他是经由她的良知而来的。”
Spurgeon said “Christ has different doors for entering into different people’s souls. Into some, he enters by the understanding; into many, by the affections. To some, he comes by the way of fear; to another, by that of hope; and to this woman he came by way of her conscience.”

传道者永远不会疲于向他人讲述关于基督的好消息。我们在服事基督时也不应感到疲倦，因为耶稣在服事我们时从未疲倦。
A evangelist is never tired of telling others all the good news about Christ. We shouldn’t feel tired when we serve Christ, as Jesus was never tired serving us.

圣经在约翰福音4:6说，当耶稣到达撒马利亚叙加的雅各井时，他因长途跋涉而困乏。那是午正六点，是一天中最热的时候。耶稣尽管疲惫炎热，仍希望能喝到清凉的水。耶稣想要服事这位撒马利亚妇人，因此通过要水喝，引出关于“活水”的对话——这水是任何人喝了都永不再渴的。
The Bible says John 4:6, when Jesus arrived Jacob’s well in Sychar Samaria, he was tired as he was from a long journey. At the sixth hour, when it was the hottest hour around noon time. Jesus, even being tired and hot, would have wanted a refreshing drink, Jesus would like to minister to such a Samaritan woman, thus asking for a drink so to lead up to a conversation about the living water that anyone should drink so he or she will never be thirsty.

这位拥有活水的主向井边索要水喝，因为他想告诉那女人关于这活水的一切。有人称泉水为活水，因为它从地里涌出，显得充满生机。但耶稣谈论的是属灵的水，它能止息属灵的渴求，并赋予属灵的生命与力量。
He who has living water asks for a drink from a well, as he likes to tell the woman all about this living water. Some people call the water from a spring living water because it seems alive as it bubble up from the ground. But Jesus was talking about the spiritual water that quenches spiritual thirst and gives spiritual life with spiritual strength.

在耶利米书2:13和17:13中，神自己被称为“活水的泉源”，所以当我们亲近神时，总能得到更新。
In Jeremiah 2:13, 17:13 God himself is called ‘the fountain of living waters’ so we can always refresh ourselves when we come close to God.

耶稣给出了一个惊人的应许：凡喝我所赐之水的，就永远不渴。神和他的话语会带给我们持续且持久的满足，这是任何人和任何事物都无法给予的。
Jesus made an amazing offer that Whoever drinks of the water that I shall give him will never thirst. God and his word would give us continuous and lasting satisfaction that no one and nothing would ever give.

有人可能会反对：“我喝了耶稣所赐的，但我还是感到干渴和空虚。我是基督徒，但我感觉不到喜乐，也不觉得充实和丰盛。”
Someone might object: “I drank of what Jesus offers, but I feel thirsty and empty again. I am a Christian, but I don’t feel the joy and I don’t feel I am full and abundant.”

朋友们，如果你认为这是一个难题，事实上答案很简单：再喝一次！对耶稣的吸取不是一次性的，不是圣经的一节或一章，不是一次崇拜或一次讲道就能永远满足的。唯有持续的敬拜、持续的研读圣经、持续地饮于圣灵，定会给我们带来喜乐、圆满、满足的生活，以及我们所能想象的一切。
Friends, if you think this is a difficult question, but in fact the answer is simple: drink again! It isn’t a one-time sip of Jesus, it isn’t a one verse or one chapter of the Bible, it isn’t a one Service or one sermon that satisfies forever, but continual worship, continual bible study, continual drink of the Holy Spirit will surely give us a life of joy, a life of fulfillment, a life of satisfaction and a life of everything we could ever imagine.

如果满足感对我们很重要，那么耶稣通过遵行他父神的旨意，拥有了更伟大的力量和满足感的源泉。耶稣的“第一且最重要的专注”（FFF）是遵行差我来者的旨意。
If satisfaction is important for us, Jesus had a greater source of strength and satisfaction by doing the will of His God and Father. Jesus’ FFF: first and foremost focus was on doing the will of Him who sent me.

当门徒以为耶稣饿了并彼此询问“莫非有人拿什么给他吃吗？”时，耶稣说：“我的食物就是遵行差我来者的旨意，作成他的工。”
When the disciples thought Jesus was hungry, and they said to each other, “Could someone have brought him food?” 34 “My food,” said Jesus, “is to do the will of him who sent me and to finish his work.

神他父的旨意是什么？耶稣做了什么来完成父的旨意？
What was God his Father’s will? What did Jesus do to finish and complete his father’s will?

不正是走那段远路去撒马利亚吗？不正是冒着因跨越边界、打破文化规则而受到嘲笑和批评的风险吗？不正是放弃站在道德高地或属灵高地上俯视、教训和谴责那些可能做错事的人的权利吗？
Was it not to walk all the way to Samaria? Was it not to risk being ridiculed and criticised for crossing the boundary, for breaking the cultural rules. Was it not to withhold the rights to stand on a moral high ground or a spiritual high ground to look down, to lecture and to reprimand those who are, maybe, doing something wrong?

对于圣大卫教堂的弟兄姊妹们，神对我们的旨意是什么？我们如何遵行神的旨意？神赐给了我们二手店、咖啡馆、英语班，神赐给了我们为新牧师祷告的责任……
For the people at St David’s, what is God’s will for us? How do we do God’s will? God has given us the thrift shop, the café, the English classes, God has given us a duty of praying for a new vicar…

让我们成就父的旨意，让我们完成被要求的任务，让我们讲述我们关于耶稣及其奇妙之爱的见证。阿门。
Let us fulfil the Father’s will, let us complete the required tasks, let us tell our testimony about Jesus and his amazing love. Amen.
