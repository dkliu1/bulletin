# Year C ALL SAINTS - November 2nd 2025
# 丙年诸圣节讲章 - 2025年11月2日

## Can you please tell me what day it is today?
## 你能告诉我今天是什么日子吗？

All Soul’s Day, and yesterday? All Saints Day, nothing to do with St Kilda football club.
今天是追思已亡节（All Souls’ Day），昨天是诸圣节（All Saints Day），与圣基尔达足球俱乐部可没有关系。

Halloween ‘trick or treat’ little monsters usually do the rounds at this time.
每到这个时候，万圣节“不给糖就捣蛋”的小怪物们就会四处游荡。

Have any of you been visited by these little monsters?
你们中有谁被这些小怪物拜访过吗？

I remember one year waiting for them, I’m happy to give them some lollies and send them on their way. Sue doesn’t like to encourage them.
我记得有一年我特意等他们来，我很乐意给他们一些糖果然后送他们走。但苏可不喜欢鼓励他们这样做。

Several years ago when I was the vicar of St Agnes, Black Rock, at 7.30 pm the door bell rang and I answered the door. No they were not Halloween Trick or Treat pranksters, worse, they were my three Church wardens.
几年前我在布莱克罗克的圣艾格尼丝教堂担任牧师。晚上七点半门铃响了，我去开门。结果来的人不是万圣节的孩子们，更糟糕——是我的三位堂区执事。

No I had not done anything bad, like wrecking the vicarage, neither had parishioners complained that my sermons were too long or boring.
不，我没干什么坏事，比如把牧师住宅弄坏，也没有教友抱怨我的讲道太长或太无聊。

My church wardens were faithfully carrying out church wardens duties like looking over parish properties to report to the AGM about matters that needed attention.
我的堂区执事们只是在忠实地履行职责，检查堂区的财产，好在年度大会上报告需要处理的事项。

Now I have a little limerick to share with you:
现在我想和你们分享一首小打油诗：

“To dwell in love with the Saints above, O what Glory, To dwell below with the Saints we know, that’s another story.”
“与天上的圣徒同居，何等荣耀；与世上的圣徒相处，那又是另一回事。”

Now if St Paul wrote a letter to the church in Moorabbin, he’d write “To the Saints in Moorabbin”.
如果圣保罗今天写信给穆拉宾教会，他会写：“致穆拉宾的圣徒们”。

OKAY, let’s name a few of them, we’d have St Jessica, St Michelle, St Marilyn, St Bob, St Wendy, St Max, St Brian, St Kerry, St Sue and St Sue and of course St Paul.
好吧，让我们来列举一些：“圣洁西卡、圣米歇尔、圣玛丽莲、圣鲍勃、圣温迪、圣马克斯、圣布莱恩、圣凯莉、圣苏，还有另一位圣苏，当然，还有圣保罗。”

Alright, now we’ve got the gist of it. “To dwell below, with the Saints we know, that’s another story,” that means we’d need to get out a can of Brasso and polish our Halo’s.
好了，我们明白了那句“与世上的圣徒相处，那又是另一回事”。这意味着我们得拿出一罐金属亮光剂来擦亮自己的光环。

Why? Because we’re not perfect, but we’re Saints in the making.
为什么呢？因为我们还不完美，但我们正在成为圣徒的路上。

Traditionally Hallows or Hallowed Eve was a time to remember dead ancestors.
传统上，万圣节前夕（All Hallows’ Eve）是纪念已逝先人的时刻。

Some would stay in cemeteries and light candles and hold a vigil in honour of the dead.
有些人会待在墓地里点燃蜡烛，为亡者守夜祈祷。

There is also a tradition, that in parts of Europe people visited cemeteries for pagan rituals...
在欧洲某些地区，还有人去墓地进行异教仪式。基督徒试图“转化”这些做法，于是在诸圣节前夕也去墓地，为的是驱赶邪恶的灵体。

It would appear they weren’t too successful...
显然，这种“转化”并不算成功，因为许多异教背景仍被保留下来。

All Souls developed in tandem with All Saints...
追思已亡节（All Souls’ Day）与诸圣节（All Saints’ Day）几乎同时发展——11月1日纪念圣人，11月2日纪念所有普通信徒。

When we speak of a ‘saint’ in the Christian church...
当我们在基督教会中谈论“圣徒”时，严格意义上指那些被“封圣”的人——他们因卓越的圣洁而被教会正式承认为在天上得享高位的人。

Saint refers to the ‘Blessed dead’...
“圣徒”也指那些蒙福的亡灵，或“天上圣徒的群体”。

The prefix ‘Saint’ is attached to many things...
“Saint”这个前缀也被用于许多有趣的名称，如城市、教堂、植物、动物甚至赛事。

So here we see the prefix ‘saint’ is generously splashed around...
可见，“Saint”这个前缀被广泛用于神圣与世俗之中。

The word Saint is derived from the Greek word ‘Hagios’, which means ‘Holy’.
“Saint”一词源自希腊语“Hagios”，意为“圣洁的”。

In the scriptures it refers to something set apart for Holy use...
在圣经中，它指被“分别为圣”的事物，用于神圣用途。

When used of God - it refers to his ‘wholly otherness’...
用于描述上帝时，它意味着“全然不同”——世上无与他相似者。

St Paul refers to all baptised Christians as ‘Hoi Hagioi’...
圣保罗称所有受洗的基督徒为“Hoi Hagioi”，即“圣洁的人们”，也就是“圣徒”。

He begins the epistle to the Ephesians, ‘to the saints in Ephesus’...
他在《以弗所书》开头写道：“致以弗所的圣徒们”。

When St Paul calls ordinary Christians saints...
当圣保罗称普通信徒为“圣徒”时，他认为他们是被上帝呼召、拣选、吸引到他面前的人。

It is not about how good we are, but about God’s initiative toward us.
这与我们自身的好坏无关，而是出于上帝的主动恩典与作为。

EPHESIANS 1:15. “I have heard of your faith in the Lord Jesus and your love toward all the saints…”
《以弗所书》1:15：“我听见你们在主耶稣里的信心，并向众圣徒的爱心……”

Paul praises their faith and love for all the saints...
保罗称赞他们对所有圣徒的信心与爱心。

The Ephesians loved not only the exemplary ones, but also the difficult ones...
以弗所人不仅爱那些模范的圣徒，也爱那些软弱、爱抱怨、挑剔、难相处的人。

By God’s grace, we are already called ‘saints’ — we just need to keep working on it.
因着上帝的恩典，我们已经被称为“圣徒”，接下来只需继续努力。

Paul prays that they may know the hope and inheritance among the saints...
保罗祈祷他们能明白上帝在圣徒中荣耀基业的盼望。

So now we have two groups of saints — the earthly saints and the heavenly ones.
因此，我们有两类圣徒：地上的圣徒与天上的圣徒。

We are saints in the present and saints in the making.
我们既是当下的圣徒，也是正在被成圣的圣徒。

We live in times when many have no vision of God’s future...
我们生活在一个许多人对上帝为他子民所预备的未来失去异象的时代。

But we are urged to hold on to this hope.
但我们被劝勉要紧紧持守这盼望。

Saints on earth — the saints at St David’s — that’s what we are.
地上的圣徒——圣大卫堂的圣徒——那就是我们。

Saints with a future, a rich and heavenly inheritance.
我们是有未来、有丰盛天国产业的圣徒。

On All Saints Day, we honour those who have entered heaven before us...
在诸圣节，我们纪念并赞美那些先我们进入天国的人。

But we also recognise the saints on earth who are still persevering.
同时也看见仍在世上奋斗、持守信仰的圣徒。

Finally, we are not alone — we have those who went before us in faith.
最后，我们并不孤单——有许多在信仰中先行的人与我们同在。

In St John’s vision, he saw “a great multitude from every nation, race, tribe and language.”
在圣约翰的异象中，他看见“从各国、各族、各民、各方来的人，多得数不清”。

Among them are those who know and love us.
其中也包括那些认识并爱我们的人。

## Let us pray:
让我们祷告：

O God our Father, help us always to see eternity beyond time.
主我们的父啊，求你帮助我们在时间之外看到永恒。

Help us to remember that we walk in the footsteps of the saints and martyrs.
帮助我们记得，我们正行走在圣徒与殉道者的脚步之中。

Grant that we may love and live as you call us to do, through Jesus Christ our Lord. Amen.
求你赐恩，使我们按你的旨意去爱、去活。奉主耶稣基督的名，阿们。
