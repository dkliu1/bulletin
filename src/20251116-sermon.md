引言 | Introduction

Brothers and sisters, today’s message comes from 2 Thessalonians 3:6–13.
弟兄姐妹，今天的信息来自 帖撒罗尼迦后书 3:6–13。

Paul speaks firmly about diligence, responsibility, and the attitude of believers toward work and service.
保罗严肃地教导我们关于勤奋、责任，以及信徒对工作与服事的态度。

经文朗读 | Scripture Reading（和合本简体）

6–13 节内容已在讲章中引用，不再重复朗读。

**一、懒惰与闲散的问题

The Problem of Idleness and Disruption**

Paul commands believers to keep away from anyone who is idle and disruptive.
保罗吩咐信徒远离那些懒惰又好制造混乱的人。

He reminds the church that he himself worked hard, day and night, never being a burden.
他提醒教会，他在他们中间日夜劳苦，不给任何人添累。

Even setting a very clear rule:
他甚至定下非常清楚的原则：

“If anyone is unwilling to work, he shall not eat.”
“若有人不肯作工，就不可吃饭。”

**二、现代社会的懒散现象

Modern Examples of Laziness**

A Stanford study suggests Indonesia walks the least steps per day.
斯坦福大学的一项研究说，印尼人每天的步数最少。

But in Australia, many people may walk even less—maybe only to the car!
但在澳洲，我们许多人走的路可能更少——可能只走到车前而已！

Yet I personally know many Indonesian friends in Australia who work extremely hard.
但我认识许多在澳洲的印尼朋友，他们非常勤奋。

Some say Switzerland is lazy because it is wealthy and enjoys good welfare and a relaxed lifestyle.
有人说瑞士懒惰，因为他们富裕、福利好、生活轻松。

Australia is similar. But we often argue that we are not lazy—we simply know how to relax.
澳洲也类似，但我们常说，我们不是懒，而是懂得享受生活。

**三、过度工作也是问题

Overworking Is Also a Problem**

Some cultures work day and night, without rest, hobbies, or time for God.
一些文化的人日夜工作，没有休息，没有兴趣爱好，也没有时间给神。

They want more money, more houses, more inheritance for future generations.
他们要更多钱、更多房子、更多遗产。

But Jesus asks in Matthew 16:26:
但耶稣在马太福音 16:26 问：

“What good is it for someone to gain the whole world, yet forfeit their soul?”
“人若赚得全世界，赔上自己的生命，有什么益处呢？”

No one should lose health, family, sabbath, worship, or their soul.
没有人该牺牲健康、家庭、安息日、敬拜，或灵魂。

**四、我是勤奋，但不是为了钱

I Work Hard, but Not for Money**

Don’t misunderstand me—I believe in hard work.
不要误会，我相信勤奋工作。

I myself am a hardworking Australian Chinese.
我自己就是个勤劳的澳洲华人。

But most of my work is not for money—
但我工作的动力并不是钱——

It is because the work of God is abundant and workers are few.
乃是因为神的工多、工人少。

**五、帖撒罗尼迦的问题：闲散的人造成破坏

The Thessalonian Issue: Idleness Causing Trouble**

Some believers were idle, unproductive, and disruptive.
有些信徒懒惰、不结果子、并且制造混乱。

They were not busy working—they were busy meddling.
他们不工作的忙，却忙着干预别人。

Busy spreading rumours, gossip, and negative emotions.
忙着传闲言、散谣言、制造不满。

**六、95% 与 5%的张力

The 95% vs. 5% Tension in the Church**

If 5% of believers cause trouble, what should the 95% do?
若 5% 的人制造麻烦，95% 的人该怎么做？

If only 5% do all the ministry while 95% fold their arms—how does a church move forward?
若只有 5% 在做所有事工，而 95% 袖手旁观，教会如何前进？

Paul is clear:
保罗讲得很清楚：

Stay away from idle and disruptive people.
要远离懒惰与好扰乱的人。

Do not let their negativity drain your spiritual energy.
不要让他们的负能量吸走你的属灵力量。

**七、向那5%至10%的忠心同工致敬

Honouring the Faithful 5–10%**

To the faithful minority who hold the church together—
向那少数但忠心、托住整个教会的人——

I salute you in the name of Christ.
我奉基督的名向你们致敬。

You are overwhelmed, burdened, carrying many duties, emails, opinions, and pressures.
你们肩负重担，被许多任务、邮件、声音、意见与压力压着。

But without you, the church cannot stand.
若没有你们，教会无法站立。

**八、保罗的劝勉：行善不可丧志

Paul’s Encouragement: Never Tire of Doing Good**

Paul says:
保罗说：

“Never tire of doing what is good.”
“行善不可丧志。”

God knows we are tired.
神知道我们累。

We want to sit like Mary, quietly listening to Jesus…
我们想像马利亚一样，静静坐着听耶稣……

But often we must work like Martha—not because we cannot sit,
但我们常必须像马大那样忙，
不是因为我们不能安静，

But because the work is undone.
而是因为工作堆积如山。

**九、继续前行：忙于事工，不忙于闲事

Keep Going: Busy for Ministry, Not for Mischief**

We must keep things running, fill vacancies, replace lost workers.
我们必须保持事工的运作，补上空缺，接替离开的工人。

Busy workers for God will be blessed.
为神忙碌的人必蒙福。

But busybodies—those who only meddle—will face consequences.
但好管闲事的人必受管教。

结语 | Conclusion

Hear my sincere word:
请听我真诚的话：

Never tire of doing what is good.
行善不可丧志。

May the Lord strengthen every faithful servant.
愿主坚固每一位忠心的仆人。

Through Jesus Christ our Lord. Amen.
奉主耶稣基督之名。阿们。
