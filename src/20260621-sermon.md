罗马书第6章与基督徒的奉献人生 / Romans 6 and the Christian Life of Giving
圣经经文 / Scripture Reading

Romans 6:1 What shall we say, then? Shall we go on sinning so that grace may increase? 2 By no means! We are those who have died to sin; how can we live in it any longer? 3 Or don’t you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death? 4 We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life.
罗马书 6:1 这样，怎么说呢？我们可以仍在罪中、叫恩典显多吗？2 断乎不可！我们在罪上死了的人，岂可仍在罪中活着呢？3 岂不知我们这受洗归入基督耶稣的人，是受洗归入他的死吗？4 所以，我们藉着洗礼归入死，和他一同埋葬，原是叫我们一举一动有新生的样式，像基督藉着父的荣耀从死里复活一样。  

5 For if we have been united with him in a death like his, we will certainly also be united with him in a resurrection like his. 6 For we know that our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled by sin might be done away with, that we should no longer be slaves to sin— 7 because anyone who has died has been set free from sin.
5 我们若在他死的形状上与他联合，也要在他复活的形状上与他联合；6 因为知道我们的旧人和他同钉十字架，使罪身灭绝，叫我们不再作罪的奴仆；7 因为已死的人是脱离了罪。  

8 Now if we died with Christ, we believe that we will also live with him. 9 For we know that since Christ was raised from the dead, he cannot die again; death no longer has mastery over him. 10 The death he died, he died to sin once for all; but the life he lives, he lives to God.
8 我们若是与基督同死，就信必与他同活。9 因为知道基督既从死里复活，就不再死，死也不再作他的主了。10 他死是向罪死了，只有一次；他活是向神活着。  

11 In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus.
11 这样，你们向罪也当看自己是死的；向神在基督耶稣里，却当看自己是活的。
信息分享 / Message Reflection

What is the one million dollar question? The question is: Should we live a life of sin so we can receive more grace?
那个价值百万美元的问题是什么？问题就是：我们是否应该活在罪恶的生活中，好让我们能获得更多的恩典？

What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin that grace may increase? Does it mean that we could sin and sin, and God would forgive and forgive, God would give us endless blessings and endless grace anyway, so it is ok that we don’t come to church, it is ok that we give God some leftover, some unwanted coins, a five dollar note perhaps, with which we can’t even get a coffee in any Mcdonald’s.
这样，怎么说呢？我们可以仍在罪中、叫恩典显多吗？这是否意味着我们可以一次又一次地犯罪，而神横竖都会一次又一次地饶恕，赐给我们无尽的福分和恩典？所以我们不来教会也没关系，把我们剩下的、不要的零钱给神也没关系——比如一张五块钱的钞票，在任何一家麦当劳甚至连一杯咖啡都买不到。

Yes I am passing on some concerns of our church giving. I know you have all done your best. But we do need to check how our giving may meet the very expensive costs of maintenance, more importantly, how our giving may reflect God’s glories and to demonstrate our gratitude to him who provides everything for us to enjoy.
是的，我正在转达对我们教会奉献状况的一些关切。我知道大家都已经尽力了。但我们确实需要审视一下，我们的奉献如何才能满足昂贵的维护成本，更重要的是，我们的奉献如何才能彰显神的荣耀，并向这位赐下万物供我们享受的神表达我们的感激之情。

We may all agree that any money business or money topics in church may upset and annoy people. People may think that this church is too secular, too commercialized, too unloving. But you know what? It blows both Marilyn and my minds… when a homeless person gives St David’s 50 dollars to thank God and all His goodness.
我们可能都会认同，在教会里谈论任何金钱事务或金钱话题都可能让人感到不快和烦恼。人们可能会认为这家教会太世俗、太商业化、太缺乏爱心。但你们知道吗？当一个无家可归的人向圣戴维斯教会捐献50美元以感谢神和祂的一切美善时，这让玛丽琳和我大为震撼。

50 dollars is a lot for a homeless person who doesn’t have a roof, who doesn’t have enough food, who doesn’t have a pair of proper shoes. So Marilyn and I decided to decline this man’s generous giving. We would urge him to keep the money for his own spend on any essentials that he needs.
对于一个没有片瓦遮身、没有足够食物、甚至没有一双合脚鞋子的流浪汉来说，50美元是一笔巨款。因此，玛丽琳和决定婉拒这位男士慷慨的奉献。我们敦促他把钱留给自己，用于购买他所需要的任何生活必需品。

As a church of course we won’t expect anyone to give beyond their means, knowing how tough and rough their life is… just a few days ago someone came to our thrift shop telling me he had less than 2 dollars in his pocket… well, I believed him and I gave him a coffee, a few chocolates and a couple of banana cakes, all for free with the love of Jesus.
作为教会，我们当然不会期望任何人超出自己的能力去奉献，因为我们深知他们的生活是多么艰难和不易……就在几天前，有人来到我们的义卖小店，告诉我他兜里只剩不到两块钱……我相信了他，凭着耶稣的爱，免费送给他一杯咖啡、几块巧克力和两块香蕉蛋糕。

So please understand that St David’s is never greedy or hunger for money, St David’s is giving a lot to the poor, to the needy people through the food bank, through our charity programs, and we never expect any financial returns. Well done, indeed, all of you!
所以请大家理解，圣戴维斯教会绝不是贪财或渴望金钱，圣戴维斯通过食物银行和我们的慈善项目，向穷人和有需要的人施予了许多，而且我们从未期待过任何经济回报。事实上，你们所有人都做得非常好！

Regarding to our offerings, we understand that God does not need our money, but He needs us to be fair, to be reasonable, to be faithful… Jesus says in Luke 12:48… "From everyone who has been given much, much will be demanded; and from the one who has been entrusted with much, much more will be asked." Jesus is talking about those who are rich, those who can afford to give more and to give better, we must give to honor God…
关于我们的奉献，我们明白神并不需要我们的钱，但祂需要我们做到公平、合理、忠心……耶稣在路加福音12:48中说：“因为多给谁，就向谁多取；多托谁，就向谁多要。” 耶稣所说的是那些富足的人，那些有能力奉献更多、奉献得更好的人，我们必须通过奉献来尊荣神……

When our treasurer is a bit surprised and she wonders why a five dollar note is found in our offering plate, I must say it: not too bad… I used to find a few 5 cent coins in my previous church’s offering plate… so we ask, is this a gift for our glorious God, or is it something, even a beggar will find it insulting and despicable.
当我们的司库有些惊讶，纳闷为什么奉献盘里会出现一张五块钱的钞票时，我必须得说：这还不算太糟……在我以前的教会里，我曾在奉献盘里发现过几枚5分的硬币……所以我们要问，这是献给我们荣耀之神的礼物呢，还是连乞丐都会觉得受到侮辱和轻视的东西？

In this context, perhaps we are reflecting: Shall we go on sinning so that grace may increase? For those who choose to give so carelessly, so disrespectful, so stingy and so mean… would they naively expect God’s appreciation, God’s forgiveness, and God’s ongoing blessings? Paul says it strongly: By no means! No way!
在这种背景下，也许我们正在反思：我们可以仍在罪中、叫恩典显多吗？对于那些选择如此马虎、如此不尊重、如此吝啬和刻薄地去奉献的人……他们难道还天真地指望得到神的赏识、神的宽恕和神不断的祝福吗？保罗强烈地表示：断乎不可！绝不可能！

Some people do believe that their job is to sin and God’s job is to forgive. If God’s salvation and approval is given on the basis of faith instead of works, won’t we just say “I believe” and then live any way we please? We may also say, I believe, and we may give anything, or not to give anything as we please.
有些人确实认为，他们的工作就是犯罪，而神的工作就是饶恕。如果神的救赎和悦纳是基于信心而不是行为，我们岂不是只要说一句“我信”，然后就可以随心所欲地生活了吗？我们也可以说“我信”，然后根据自己的喜好随意奉献，或者什么都不奉献。

Paul says No! We need to realize that God doesn’t like us to abuse anything, any resources, any authority, any rights, any privileges. We need to appreciate, we need to cherish, but never waste, never take it for granted, never abuse God’s grace, God’s tolerance and God’s patience.
保罗说：不！我们需要认识到，神不喜欢我们滥用任何东西，无论是任何资源、任何权柄、任何权利还是任何特权。我们需要感激，我们需要珍惜，但绝不能浪费，绝不能视为理所当然，绝不能滥用神的恩典、神的宽容和神的忍耐。

The Bible is using a present active sense for the phrase: "Shall we continue in sin": this makes it clear that Paul is describing and denouncing the practice of habitual sin. Paul is talking about someone who remains in a lifestyle of sin, thinking that it is acceptable and the grace may still increase.
圣经在“仍在罪中”这个短语中使用了现在时态主动语态，这清楚地表明保罗是在描述并谴责习惯性犯罪的行为。保罗所指的是那些沉溺于罪恶生活方式之中的人，他们还以为这是可以接受的，恩典依然会增多。

It is so important for Christians to form some good habits, habit to go to church regularly, habit to serve diligently, habit to give generously and selflessly. We are called to die to sin so we no longer live in sin, we no longer live to fall short of God’s standard and God’s requirements.
对于基督徒来说，养成一些好习惯是如此重要：定期去教会的习惯、勤勉服侍的习惯、慷慨无私奉献的习惯。我们蒙召是要在罪上死，使我们不再活在罪中，不再活在亏缺神的标准和要求之中。

For Paul, the idea that anyone might continue in sin that grace may increase is unthinkable. "Certainly not" is a strong phrase. It might also be translated, “Perish the thought!” Or, “Away with the notion!” Through baptism, we become a new creation and we receive the Holy Spirit. Therefore we were buried with Him through baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead.
在保罗看来，任何人为了让恩典显多而继续犯罪的想法都是不可思议的。“断乎不可”是一个强烈的词组。它也可以翻译为“打消这个念头！”或“摒弃这种想法！”通过洗礼，我们成为新造的人，并领受了圣灵。因此，我们藉着洗礼归入死，和他一同埋葬，原是叫我们像基督从死里复活一样。

So something dramatic and life changing happened in the life of a baptised Christian. We can’t die and rise again without our life transformation. Our old self is already dead because of our identification with Jesus’ death on the cross.
因此，在受洗的基督徒生命中，发生了一些戏剧性的、改变生命的重大改变。如果我们的生命没有发生蜕变，我们就不能算作与基督同死同复活。由于我们认同耶稣在十字架上的死，我们的旧人就已经死了。

Romans 6:10 The death he died, he died to sin once for all; but the life he lives, he lives to God. 11 In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus.
罗马书 6:10 他死是向罪死了，只有一次；他活是向神活着。11 这样，你们向罪也当看自己是死的；向神在基督耶稣里，却当看自己是活的。

In Jesus, we are truly set free from sin, we are free and alive to please God and honor God in every aspect of our life, including serving, including giving. Yes God is forever gracious, God is forever merciful, but grace and mercy is not a licence for us to sin, grace and mercy is in fact a call to a transformed life, a call to give as God bless a cheerful giver, and it is more blessed to give than to receive.
在耶稣里，我们真正从罪中得到了释放，我们在自由中活着，在生命的每个方面去讨神的喜悦并尊荣神，这包括服侍，也包括奉献。是的，神永远有恩典，神永远有怜悯，但恩典和怜悯并不是我们犯罪的许可证；相反，恩典和怜悯是向蜕变生命的呼唤，是呼唤我们去奉献，因为神赐福给捐得乐意的人，并且施比受更为有福。

Living under grace enables us to overcome sin, to stop indulging in sin, to give ourselves no more excuses in sinning. The grace of God compels us to be authentic and prevents us from being nominal. We are not Christians in name only. True Christianity condemns a lukewarm faith and it demands a life that reflects Christ’s teachings.
活在恩典之下使能够我们战胜罪恶，停止沉溺于罪中，不再为自己的犯罪找借口。神的恩典促使我们追求真实，防止我们流于形式。我们不只是名义上的基督徒。真正的基督教信仰谴责不冷不热的信心，它要求我们的生命必须反映出基督的教导。

I said it many times and before my departure next Sunday, let me say it again, St David’s has done very well in serving Christ. You all have done very well as you acknowledge Christ and you have taken up your cross to follow Christ. Certainly you are generous and gracious as you know God is always generous and gracious. But it won’t harm to reflect more, to revise more, how a healthier offering may help to fund and advance further Christ’s Gospel ministry in Moorabbin.
我已经说过很多次了，在下周日我离开之前，让我再说一次：圣戴维斯教会在服侍基督方面做得非常好。你们承认基督，并背起自己的十字架来跟随基督，你们都做得非常好。你们当然是慷慨和仁慈的，因为你们知道神总是慷慨和仁慈的。但多一些反思、多一些审视并无坏处，看看更健康的奉献如何能有助于资助并进一步推进基督在莫拉宾（Moorabbin）的福音事工。
结束祷告 / Closing Prayer

Let us pray:
我们同心祷告：

Father God, you know that we are all sinners and we all fall short of your glory. We may have failed to give more generously that is how our church finance is a bit worrying. Please forgive us if this has been a sin against you. Please help us to give more and to give better. Please provide us some financial resources through your faithful people, so we may have capacity to start and consolidate more outreach and evangelistic work for the lost. In Jesus’ Name, Amen.
父神，你知道我们都是罪人，都亏缺了你的荣耀。我们可能未能更加慷慨地奉献，以至于我们教会的财务状况有些令人担忧。如果这成了得罪你的罪，求你赦免我们。请帮助我们奉献得更多、奉献得更好。求你藉着你忠心的子民为我们提供资金资源，使我们有能力为迷失的人开展并巩固更多的外展和传道工作。奉耶稣的名求，阿们。
