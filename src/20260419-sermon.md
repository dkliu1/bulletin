Year A - EASTER 3 | 19/4/26
A年 - 复活期第三主日 | 2026年4月19日
Readings 经课

Acts 2:14a, 22-32 (使徒行传 2:14a, 22-32)
Psalm 116:1-4, 11-18 (诗篇 116:1-4, 11-18)
1 Peter 1:13-25 (彼得前书 1:13-25)
Luke 24:13-35 (路加福音 24:13-35)

Some years ago while I was still the vicar at St Agnes’ Black Rock, I went back to India after almost 24 years. I let only a few relatives know I was coming - as for the rest of my very vast circle of friends and acquaintances all over Bangalore - no one knew I was coming.
几年前，当我还在黑石圣艾格尼丝教堂担任教区牧师时，我在阔别近24年后回到了印度。我只告诉了少数亲戚我要回去的消息——至于我在班加罗尔那庞大的朋友和熟人圈子，没有人知道我要去。

I went to church on Advent Sunday, and over the week to a number of places where there were people who had known me for years.
我在待降节的主日去了教堂，在那一周里，我也去了好几个地方，那里的人都认识我多年。

It was an interesting experience. I looked for and recognised most people. However not all recognised me - a few looked at me - and carried on with whatever they were doing, and then looked up again - as though I reminded them of someone they knew - and sometimes it led to recognition, and great surprise - while there were others looked at me and nothing connected at all - simply because it had been so long - 24 years, a quarter of a century, and no one was expecting me to be there.
那是一次有趣的经历。我寻找并认出了大多数人。然而，并不是所有人都认出了我——有些人看了看我，继续做他们手头的事，然后又抬起头来——仿佛我让他们想起了一个熟人——有时这会触发认出后的惊喜。而另一些人看着我，却完全没有任何联想——仅仅是因为时间太久了，24年，四分之一个世纪，没有人预料到我会出现在那里。

Then one day I turned up at my old school, SJHS, I recognised a childhood friend - her name is Grace, I knew Grace and her family from Primary School days, we went to Sunday School, sang in the choir and were members of the Church youth group.
后来有一天，我出现在我的母校 SJHS，我认出了一位儿时的朋友——她叫格蕾丝（Grace）。我从小学时代就认识格蕾丝和她的家人，我们一起上主日学，在唱诗班唱歌，也是教会青年团契的成员。

I called out to her, she spun around at hearing my voice, came running up hugged me and kissed me with delight - in public - not the kind of thing Indians do - in public - she couldn’t contain her joy - there were tears in her eyes.
我大声呼唤她，她听到我的声音猛地转过身，跑过来热情地拥抱我、亲吻我——在公共场合——这通常不是印度人会在公开场合做的事——她无法抑制内心的喜悦，眼中含着泪水。

Then she told me her story. She had married a Hindu boy, from school - who assured her that their different faiths would pose no problem for their marriage.
接着她告诉我她的故事。她嫁给了一个学校里的印度教男孩，那个男孩曾向她保证，他们不同的信仰不会给婚姻带来问题。

Now she told me, she was allowed to go to church only for Christmas and Easter. She had to give up the choir and all other church related activities. Her two daughters were not allowed to go to church or Sunday school, they were brought up as Hindus.
现在她告诉我，她只被允许在圣诞节和复活节去教堂。她不得不放弃唱诗班和所有其他与教会相关的活动。她的两个女儿不被允许去教堂或主日学，她们是作为印度教徒被抚养长大的。

She had to wear the ‘Bindu’ - the identifying Hindu mark on her forehead - which Christians would never wear - because that is where they are signed with the sign of the cross at their Baptism. For Christians it is tantamount to a denial of their faith and Christian identity.
她必须在额头上佩戴“宾得”（Bindu）——印度教的标识——这是基督徒绝不会佩戴的，因为那是他们在受洗时被画上十字架印记的地方。对基督徒来说，这等同于否认自己的信仰和基督徒身份。

After we parted - I saw her once more on Christmas Day as she walked up aisle for Holy Communion - sadness written all over her faith - I never saw Grace again. Interestingly she got hold of my WhatsApp number and communicates with me every day. I often send her little Christian quotes or other Christian messages along with other humorous quotes and stories.
我们分别后，我在圣诞节那天又见过她一次，当时她正走向祭坛领圣餐——忧伤写满了她的脸庞——之后我再也没见过格蕾丝。有趣的是，她拿到了我的 WhatsApp 号码，每天都与我交流。我经常给她发一些基督教短语或其他信仰信息，以及一些幽默的语录和故事。

The joy of recognition and renewed friendship after 24 years was a very special occasion, but also a very sad one.
这种阔别24年后的相认与重逢的喜悦是非常特别的，但同时也是非常忧伤的。

Today’s Gospel is the narrative of the recognition of the resurrected Jesus, and it took quite sometime before they were able to recognise him - It was one encounter they were not expecting or prepared for.
今天的福音书讲述了人们认出复活耶稣的经过，他们花了相当长的一段时间才认出他来——这是一次他们始料未及、也未曾准备好的邂逅。

This event took place on the same day our Lord rose from the grave - Easter Day but late in the afternoon.
这件事发生在主从坟墓里复活的同一天——复活节，但在傍晚时分。

Today is the third Sunday of Easter - and we’re still dwelling on Easter Day events - whereas in the secular community and supermarkets, Easter eggs and bunnies went off the shelves shortly after Easter or were reduced to clear at half price.
今天是复活期第三个主日——我们仍在回味复活节当天的事件——而在世俗社会和超市里，复活节彩蛋和兔子在复活节后不久就下架了，或者减价一半清仓处理。

Easter has been promptly forgotten and Footy paraphernalia now takes up lots of shelf space. Here in God’s church we do not let the significance of this day and this event - the Resurrection - to pass away so casually. We dwell on it, let it sink in and it’s reality penetrate our lives. Because he lives, we live.
复活节很快就被遗忘了，足球装备现在占据了大量的货架空间。但在上帝的教会中，我们不让这一天和这一事件——复活——的意义就这样随意流逝。我们沉思它，让它沉淀，让它的真实性渗透我们的生活。因为他活着，我们也活着。

The Church is still focused on that one day, that first Easter Day, a fortnight later. And WHY NOT? The resurrection is such a stupendous event, it is an event and a day that made a difference.
两周后，教会仍然聚焦于那一天，那个最初的复活节。为什么不呢？复活是如此惊天动地的大事，是一个改变了世界的日子。

The Emmaus road encounter. One of the two men on the road to Emmaus was called Cleopas - according to one tradition he was the brother of Joseph - supposed father of our Lord. Emmaus was about eleven kilometres or over two hours from Jerusalem.
以马忤斯路上的邂逅。在去以马忤斯路上的两个人中，有一个名叫革流巴——根据一种传统，他是约瑟（我主的养父）的兄弟。以马忤斯距离耶路撒冷约11公里，步行需两个多小时。

There is no mention as to why they were going to Emmaus - whether they were going to friends or family or simply to get away from the place of such painful experiences. As they walked they discussed with each other the happenings of the past week.
经文没有提到他们为什么去以马忤斯——是去探亲访友，还是仅仅为了逃离那个充满了痛苦经历的地方。他们走着的时候，彼此讨论着过去一周发生的事情。

It wouldn’t have been easy for them to discuss these matters with anyone in Jerusalem at the time for fear of the Jews - too many of Jesus’ enemies were around. So when they were out of people’s hearing they could talk to each other with greater freedom.
当时，由于惧怕犹太人，他们在耶路撒冷很难与任何人讨论这些事——耶稣的敌人太多了。所以当他们避开众人的耳目时，他们可以更自由地交谈。

From their conversation it is clear they were talking about the possibility of his resurrection, and yet, at the same time confused and disappointed - hoping against hope.
从他们的谈话中可以清楚地看到，他们在谈论他复活的可能性，但与此同时又感到困惑和失望——在绝望中仍存留一丝希望。

As they walked Jesus himself drew near and joined them, but they did not recognise him.
他们走路的时候，耶稣亲自走近他们，和他们同行，但他们没有认出他。

Perhaps they were so overcome by a sense of hopelessness and despair, by the events of Good Friday, that they didn’t expect to see Jesus alive - perhaps they walked with their eyes downcast - looking at the ground - like people people do when they are discouraged and feel all is hopeless.
也许是因为受难日的事件让他们深陷于绝望和沮丧，以至于他们压根没指望能见到活着的耶稣——也许他们低着头走路，眼睛盯着地面——就像那些灰心丧气、感到万念俱灰的人一样。

The interesting thing is that our Lord does not attempt to reveal his identity to them, then and there. “Hey guys! It’s me, Jesus, here look at me, don’t look so downhearted.”
有趣的是，我们的主当时并没有试图向他们透露自己的身份。他没有说：“嘿，伙计们！是我，耶稣，看我，别垂头丧气的。”

Here already on Easter Day - Day One we see our Lord clearly establishing the means of discovery and his self revelation - for the Church of the future - FOR US!
早在复活节的第一天，我们就看到主为未来的教会——为我们！——清晰地确立了发现他并让他自我显露的方式。

His disciples had for so long depended upon his bodily presence. - now the time had come to wean them away from that dependence - to teach them and lead them to discover his SPIRITUAL PRESENCE with them by other means - the means provided for FAITH.
他的门徒长期以来一直依赖他肉身的同在。现在，是时候让他们脱离这种依赖了——去教导并引领他们，通过其他方式来发现他与他们同在的“属灵同在”——即为信心所预备的方式。

So we see our Lord opening up the Scriptures to them, beginning with the writings of Moses, and showing them how they were fulfilled in Jesus of Nazareth. A walking Bible study, led by our Lord himself.
于是我们看到主向他们开启经卷，从摩西的著作开始，向他们展示这些话如何在拿撒勒人耶稣身上得以应验。这是一场由主亲自带领的、行走中的查经会。

The two men were so deeply engrossed by what he showed them (“were not our hearts burning within us while he was talking to us on the road”).
这两个人被他所展示的内容深深吸引了（“在路上，他虽然和我们说话，给我们讲解圣经的时候，我们的心岂不是火热的吗？”）。

Then they came to their journey’s end, and he still had not revealed his identity to them. Jesus acted as if he would continue on His journey - but they seized the opportunity to invite him to spend the night at their place, he accepted their invitation and went in.
后来他们走到了旅程的终点，他仍然没有向他们透露身份。耶稣表现得好像还要往前走——但他们抓住机会邀请他在他们那里过夜，他接受了邀请，进去了。

The evening meal was ready and served, and in a totally unexpected turn of events - the visitor assumed the place of the host by taking the bread, blessing it and breaking it.
晚餐准备好了，呈了上来。在完全意想不到的转折中——这位客旅反客为主，拿起饼来，祝福了，掰开。

At that moment, Luke tells us, ‘their eyes were opened and they recognised him’. In that instant hope was revived and faith restored.
路加告诉我们，就在那一刻，“他们的眼睛明亮了，这才认出他来”。那一瞬间，希望重燃，信心得以恢复。

The emphasis in Luke’s narrative is not on the wonder of the fact of the resurrection, or the joyful recognition of the Lord - but the method of recognition - how he came to be recognised by the disciples, and this is important for us.
路加叙述的重点不在于复活这一事实的神奇，也不在于认出主后的喜悦——而在于认出的“方式”——他是如何被门徒认出来的，这对我们很重要。

Luke’s narrative has a specific teaching purpose - to teach the church something - HOW WE ENCOUNTER THE LORD.
路加的叙述有一个特定的教学目的——教导教会一件事——我们如何遇见主。

After the Reformation, it is said people would look inside a church to take note of the Pulpit and Altar, to see which was given the greater prominence.
据说在宗教改革之后，人们进入教堂会留意讲道坛（Pulpit）和祭坛（Altar），看看哪一个更受重视。

If the pulpit was more prominent - it indicated that this church was given to the preaching of the word SOLA SCRIPTURA. It said to them correct doctrine and teaching is important here, and as long as you know and understand your doctrine correctly, that’s the most important thing.
如果讲道坛更突出——这表明这座教堂致力于“唯独圣经”的圣言宣讲。它向人们传递出：在这里，正确的教义和教导是重要的，只要你正确地了解和理解教义，那就是最重要的事。

If the altar was given greater prominence - it indicated a greater sacramental emphasis. Preaching and teaching was not that important, it said to them preaching and teaching may not be that important, it doesn’t matter too much what you know or believe, as long as you receive the sacrament regularly.
如果祭坛更受重视——这表明更强调圣礼。讲道和教导并不那么重要，它向人们传达：讲道和教导可能没那么要紧，你了解什么或相信什么并不太重要，只要你定期领受圣礼即可。

That was the difference between the papists and the reformers. In the light of today’s Gospel this is a false dichotomy. It illustrates that word and sacrament are equally important in making Christ known to us.
那是当时教皇派与改革派的区别。但根据今天的福音书，这是一种错误的对立。它说明了圣言和圣礼在让我们认识基督方面具有同等的重要性。

The disciples said, “were not our hearts burning within us while he was talking to us on the road” Jesus was expounding the OT to them. Then their eyes were opened and they recognised him in the breaking of the bread.
门徒说：“在路上，他……给我们讲解圣经的时候，我们的心岂不是火热的吗？”耶稣当时正在向他们阐释旧约。然后，在掰饼的时候，他们的眼睛明亮了，认出了他。

St Augustine spoke of the reading and exposition of the Scriptures as audible word of God, and the Lord’s Supper and Baptism as the visible word of God.
圣奥古斯丁将圣经的宣读和阐释称为“听得见的上帝之道”，而将圣餐和洗礼称为“看得见的上帝之道”。

The disciples found faith as they heard the Scriptures explained, and as Jesus broke the bread. The two means of Grace, means of encountering Jesus, handed down to us by our Lord himself.
门徒在听闻讲解圣经和耶稣掰饼时找到了信心。这是两种恩典的媒介，是遇见耶稣的方式，由主亲自传承给我们。

For us any and every Sunday is a little Easter, and a day of discovering the resurrected Jesus, as we encounter him in Word and Sacrament.
对我们来说，每一个主日都是一个小复活节，是我们在圣言与圣礼中遇见并发现复活耶稣的日子
