约翰福音 11:1-45 / John 11:1-45

有一个患病的人，名叫拉撒路，住在伯大尼，就是马利亚和她姐姐马大居住的村庄。
Now a man named Lazarus was sick. He was from Bethany, the village of Mary and her sister Martha.

这马利亚就是那用香膏抹主，又用头发擦他脚的；患病的拉撒路是她的兄弟。
(This Mary, whose brother Lazarus now lay sick, was the same one who poured perfume on the Lord and wiped his feet with her hair.)

她两个姐妹就打发人去见耶稣，说：“主啊，你所爱的人病了。”
So the sisters sent word to Jesus, “Lord, the one you love is sick.”

耶稣听见就说：“这病不至于死，乃是为上帝的荣耀，叫上帝的儿子因此得荣耀。”
When he heard this, Jesus said, “This sickness will not end in death. No, it is for God’s glory so that God’s Son may be glorified through it.”

耶稣素来爱马大和他妹子并拉撒路。
Now Jesus loved Martha and her sister and Lazarus.

听见拉撒路病了，就在所居之地仍住了两天。
So when he heard that Lazarus was sick, he stayed where he was two more days,

然后对门徒说：“我们再往犹太去吧。”
and then he said to his disciples, “Let us go back to Judea.”

门徒说：“拉比，犹太人近来要拿石头打你，你还往那里去吗？”
“But Rabbi,” they said, “a short while ago the Jews there tried to stone you, and yet you are going back?”

耶稣回答说：“白日不是有十二小时吗？人在白日走路，就不至跌倒，因为看见这世上的光。
Jesus answered, “Are there not twelve hours of daylight? Anyone who walks in the daytime will not stumble, for they see by this world’s light.

若在黑夜走路，就必跌倒，因为他身上没有光。”
It is when a person walks at night that they stumble, for they have no light.”

耶稣说了这话，随后对他们说：“我们的朋友拉撒路睡着了，我去叫醒他。”
After he had said this, he went on to tell them, “Our friend Lazarus has fallen asleep; but I am going there to wake him up.”

门徒说：“主啊，他若睡着了，就必好了。”
His disciples replied, “Lord, if he sleeps, he will get better.”

耶稣这话是指着他死说的，他们却以为是说照常睡了。
Jesus had been speaking of his death, but his disciples thought he meant natural sleep.

耶稣就明明地告诉他们说：“拉撒路死了。
So then he told them plainly, “Lazarus is dead,

我没有在那里就欢喜，这是为你们的缘故，好叫你们相信。如今我们可以往他那里去吧。”
and for your sake I am glad I was not there, so that you may believe. But let us go to him.”

多马，又名低土马，就对那同作门徒的说：“我们也去和他同死吧。”
Then Thomas (also known as Didymus) said to the rest of the disciples, “Let us also go, that we may die with him.”

耶稣到了，就知道拉撒路在坟墓里已经四天了。
On his arrival, Jesus found that Lazarus had already been in the tomb for four days.

伯大尼离耶路撒冷不远，约有六里路。
Now Bethany was less than two miles from Jerusalem,

有好些犹太人来看马大和马利亚，要为她们兄弟的缘故安慰她们。
and many Jews had come to Martha and Mary to comfort them in the loss of their brother.

马大听见耶稣来了，就出去迎接他；马利亚却仍在家里坐着。
When Martha heard that Jesus was coming, she went out to meet him, but Mary stayed at home.

马大对耶稣说：“主啊，你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死。
“Lord,” Martha said to Jesus, “if you had been here, my brother would not have died.

就是现在，我也知道，你无论向上帝求什么，上帝也必赐给你。”
But I know that even now God will give you whatever you ask.”

耶稣说：“你兄弟必然复活。”
Jesus said to her, “Your brother will rise again.”

马大说：“我知道在末日复活的时候，他必复活。”
Martha answered, “I know he will rise again in the resurrection at the last day.”

耶稣对她说：“复活在我，生命也在我。信我的人虽然死了，也必复活；
Jesus said to her, “I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die;

凡活着信我的人必永远不死。你信这话吗？”
and whoever lives by believing in me will never die. Do you believe this?”

马大说：“主啊，是的，我信你是基督，是上帝的儿子，就是那要临到世界的。”
“Yes, Lord,” she replied, “I believe that you are the Messiah, the Son of God, who is to come into the world.”

马大说了这话，就回去暗暗地叫她妹子马利亚，说：“夫子来了，叫你。”
After she had said this, she went back and called her sister Mary aside. “The Teacher is here,” she said, “and is asking for you.”

马利亚听见了，就急忙起来，到耶稣那里去。
When Mary heard this, she got up quickly and went to him.

那时耶稣还没有进村子，仍在马大迎接他的地方。
Now Jesus had not yet entered the village, but was still at the place where Martha had met him.

那些同马利亚在家里安慰她的犹太人，见她急忙起来出去，就跟着她，以为她要往坟墓那里去哭。
When the Jews who had been with Mary in the house, comforting her, noticed how quickly she got up and went out, they followed her, supposing she was going to the tomb to mourn there.

马利亚到了耶稣那里，看见他，就俯伏在他脚前，说：“主啊，你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死。”
When Mary reached the place where Jesus was and saw him, she fell at his feet and said, “Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died.”

耶稣看见她哭，并看见与她同来的犹太人也哭，就心里悲叹，又甚忧愁，
When Jesus saw her weeping, and the Jews who had come along with her also weeping, he was deeply moved in spirit and troubled.

便说：“你们把他安放在哪里？”他们回答说：“请主来看。”
“Where have you laid him?” he asked. “Come and see, Lord,” they replied.

耶稣哭了。
Jesus wept.

犹太人就说：“你看他爱这人是何等恳切！”
Then the Jews said, “See how he loved him!”

其中有人说：“他既然开了瞎子的眼睛，岂不能叫这人不死吗？”
But some of them said, “Could not he who opened the eyes of the blind man have kept this man from dying?”

耶稣又心里悲叹，来到坟墓前；那坟墓是个洞，有一块石头挡着。
Jesus, once more deeply moved, came to the tomb. It was a cave with a stone laid across the entrance.

耶稣说：“把石头挪开。”那死人的姐姐马大对他说：“主啊，他现在必是臭了，因为他死了已经四天了。”
“Take away the stone,” he said. “But, Lord,” said Martha, the sister of the dead man, “by this time there is a bad odor, for he has been there four days.”

耶稣说：“我不是对你说过，你若信，就必看见上帝的荣耀吗？”
Then Jesus said, “Did I not tell you that if you believe, you will see the glory of God?”

他们就把石头挪开。耶稣举目望天，说：“父啊，我感谢你，因为你已经听我。
So they took away the stone. Then Jesus looked up and said, “Father, I thank you that you have heard me.

我也知道你常听我，但我说这话是为周围站着的众人，叫他们信是你差了我来。”
I knew that you always hear me, but I said this for the benefit of the people standing here, that they may believe that you sent me.”

说了这话，就大声呼叫说：“拉撒路出来！”
When he had said this, Jesus called in a loud voice, “Lazarus, come out!”

那死人就出来了，手脚裹着布，脸上包着手巾。
The dead man came out, his hands and feet wrapped with strips of linen, and a cloth around his face.

耶稣对他们说：“解开，让他走！”
Jesus said to them, “Take off the grave clothes and let him go.”

那些来看马利亚的犹太人见了耶稣所做的事，就多有信他的。
Therefore many of the Jews who had come to visit Mary, and had seen what Jesus did, believed in him.
灵修与讲道反思 / Reflections and Sermon Notes

一个从未去过教会的人突然出现在教堂里，在牧师准备讲道时站起来对他大喊大叫：“你这个伪君子！昨晚凌晨3点我打电话请你去为我病重的母亲祈祷，你竟然没有立刻赶来。现在她死了，就在凌晨4点去世了。你现在竟然在这里讲道，而不去安慰丧亲的家属，你怎敢自称牧师！”
Someone who had never been to church before, suddenly turned up in church and he stood up and yelled at the pastor when he was ready to preach. ‘You hypocrite, how dare you as a pastor you didn’t come to visit my mother right away when I called you at 3am last night to pray for her recovery, now she died, she died at 4am and you are here preaching a sermon but not going to comfort the bereaved family!’

如果你觉得这是一个虚构的故事……你是对的。我编造这个故事是为了夸大现实中可能发生的情况。
Well, if you think this is a make up story… you are right… I make this up to exaggerate a little what could actually happen.

但我并没有胡编乱造的是：曾有不止一次，有人要求我去医院探望他们垂死的家人……我确实去了，但当我赶到并询问病人情况时，被告知病人已经去世了。
I am not making up when I say that more than a few times, someone asked me to go visiting their dying family members in the hospital… I did go but by the time I managed to arrive and asked for the patient, I was told the patient had already died.

每一次我都为家属和死者感到非常难过，但我承认，任何牧师、神职人员都有许多局限，可能无法全天候满足所有紧急需求。尽管我们受召服侍，但我们只是凡人。不像耶稣，甚至即便是耶稣，他也没有在拉撒路病重时立刻回应马大和马利亚的请求去医治他。为什么？是耶稣太忙了吗？还是耶稣太冷漠了？
Each time I feel so so sorry for the family, for the deceased, but I admit and confess, there are a lot of limits for any priests, any clergy who might not be available around the clock for all urgent needs and requests. Even though we are called and charged into ministry, but we are only humans, unlike Jesus, or even Jesus, he could not, or he did not respond to Martha and Mary’s request to visit and heal Lazarus right away, even when he was very sick. Why? Was Jesus that busy, or was Jesus that indifferent?

查尔斯·斯布真（Charles Spurgeon）对约翰福音 11 章的评论强调，耶稣延迟医治拉撒路表明，爱允许苦难发生以彰显上帝的荣耀。他聚焦于“耶稣哭了”（约 11:35）中蕴含的“最深邃的海洋”，突显了基督的人性共情、他对死亡的权柄，以及一个真理：他所爱的人即便患病，在他的看顾下也是绝对安全的。
Charles Spurgeon’s commentary on John 11 emphasizes that Jesus’s delay in healing Lazarus demonstrates that love allows suffering for God’s glory. He focuses on the "deepest ocean" within "Jesus wept" (John 11:35), highlighting Christ's human empathy, his power over death, and the truth that those He loves can be both ill and perfectly safe in His care.

斯布真教导说，祷告未获速应并非拒绝，而是为了上帝的荣耀。爱有时需要经历在那时看来痛苦的延迟，但这是为了达成更高的目的。
Spurgeon teaches that delays in answered prayer are not denials but are for the glory of God. Love sometimes necessitates a delay that seems painful at the time, but serves a higher purpose.

正如我们上周从《约翰福音》第 9 章听到的，上帝的作为在生来瞎眼的人身上彰显。
God’s work displayed, when a man born blind as we heard from John 9, last week’s gospel reading.

今天我们听到：当耶稣拒绝立刻医治拉撒路时，上帝的荣耀得以彰显，上帝的儿子也因此得荣耀。耶稣推迟去看拉撒路，直到他死去、尸体躺在坟墓里。然后耶稣使死去了四天的拉撒路复活。他施行的是复活神迹而非简单的医治，展现了对死亡的绝对权威。
Today we hear: God’s glory displayed, and God’s son gloried, when Jesus declined to heal Lazarus right away, Jesus delayed going to see Lazarus until Lazarus died, the dead body was lying in the tomb, then Jesus raised him from his death, after Lazarus had been dead for four days, he performed a resurrection rather than a mere healing, showing absolute authority over death.

在一世纪的犹太信仰中，人们认为灵魂会在尸体附近逗留三天。到了第四天，身体开始腐烂，死亡被认为是不可逆转的。耶稣在第四天到达，无可置疑地证明了拉撒路是真正死亡了，而不仅仅是昏迷或熟睡。
In first-century Jewish belief, the soul was thought to linger near the body for three days. By the fourth day, physical decay had begun, and death was considered irreversible. Arriving on day four proved beyond question that Lazarus was truly dead and not merely in a coma or deep sleep.

尽管姐妹俩发出了紧急信息：“主啊，你所爱的人病了。”（约 11:3）耶稣的反应却似乎令人难以接受。他没有说“天哪，我马上过去”，而是给出了一个看似无关且令人失望的回答。
Despite the sisters’ urgent message: “Lord, the one You love is sick.” (John 11:3) • Jesus’ response was quite unacceptable.. instead of saying, Oh dear I am coming over right now…. Jesus’s answer was in fact irrelevant and disappointing.

耶稣说：“这病不至于死，乃是为上帝的荣耀，叫上帝的儿子因此得荣耀。”（约 11:4）
Jesus said “This sickness will not end in death, but it is for the glory of God, so that the Son of God may be glorified through it.” (John 11:4)

耶稣没有立刻提供帮助，他没有动身，似乎只是给了一个虚假的希望，保证拉撒路不会死。他继续做着手头的事：“听见拉撒路病了，就在所居之地仍住了两天。”（约 11:6）
Jesus didn’t help right away, he didn’t go, and it seems he just gave a false hope assuring Lazarus won’t die, and he kept doing what he was doing “So on hearing that Lazarus was sick, He stayed where He was for two days.” (John 11:6)

马大和马利亚称拉撒路为“耶稣所爱的人”。基于这种亲密关系，她们期望耶稣能赶来救助并医治她们生病的兄弟，但耶稣没有。因此，很难理解为什么耶稣对他所爱的人反应如此冷淡。
The sisters Mary and Martha referred to Lazarus as the one whom Jesus loves. With this close relationship, they expected Jesus to rush to their aid and heal their sick brother, but Jesus did not. It is therefore difficult to understand why Jesus seems to give such a lukewarm response, even to his loved ones.

让我们试着理解耶稣有意的耽延。拉撒路和他的家人住在伯大尼。当拉撒路生病的消息传到耶稣那里时，耶稣正在约旦河外的伯大巴喇。我们知道那里是施洗约翰曾开展基础工作、呼吁人们为耶稣预备道路的重要服侍地。
Let us try to understand Jesus deliberate procrastination. Lazarus and his family lived in Bethany. When the news of Lazarus sickness reached Jesus, Jesus was at the place called Bethabara beyond the Jordan. We would understand the important ministry there around River Jordan where John the Baptist had started some grounded work by asking people to prepare themselves for Jesus.

伯大尼和伯大巴喇之间的距离约为 15 到 20 英里，即一天的路程。收到消息后，耶稣为了约旦地区的必要服侍又在那里停留了两天。当耶稣到达伯大尼时，被告知拉撒路已经死四天了。
The distance between Bethany and Bethabara is around 15 to 20 miles, i.e. a day’s journey. After receiving the news Jesus stayed there 2 more days for the necessary ministry in Jordan region. When Jesus reached Bethany, He was told that it had been 4 days since Lazarus died.

耶稣到达伯大尼时，马利亚埋怨道：“主啊，你若早在这里，我兄弟必不死。”当耶稣来到拉撒路的坟墓并要求人们挪开入口的石头时，马大埋怨道：“主啊，他现在必是臭了，因为他死了已经四天了。”
When Jesus arrived Bethany, Mary complained “Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died.” And when Jesus went to Lazarus’ tomb and asked people to take the entrance stone away, Martha complained, “But, Lord,” said Martha, “by this time there is a bad odor, for he has been there four days.”

耶稣说：“我不是对你说过，你若信，就必看见上帝的荣耀吗？”（约 11:40）
40 Then Jesus said, “Did I not tell you that if you believe, you will see the glory of God?” (John 11:40)

当我们最在上帝里得满足时，上帝最得荣耀。当我们的信心坚固并不断增强时，上帝得荣耀。耶稣的延迟是有意的、有目的的，旨在深化每个人的信心。
God is best glorified when we are best satisfied in Him. God is glorified when our faith is strong and getting stronger. Jesus’ delay was deliberate, was purposeful, was aiming at deepen everyone’s faith …

耶稣说：“拉撒路死了。我没有在那里就欢喜，这是为你们的缘故，好叫你们相信。”（约 11:14-15）
Jesus said “Lazarus is dead, and for your sake I am glad I was not there, so that you may believe.” 11: 14-15

许多犹太人聚集在一起安慰马大和马利亚，因为拉撒路死了（约 11:19）。耶稣推迟了他的使命直到拉撒路去世，而复活的神迹使旁观者的信仰倍增，从而为上帝带来了更大的荣耀。
Many Jews had gathered to comfort Martha and Mary because Lazarus died. (John 11:19). Jesus’ delayed his mission till Lazarus died and the miracle of resurrection multiplied belief among onlookers, thus brought a greater glory for God..

“因此，那些来看马利亚并见耶稣所做之事的犹太人，多有信他的。”（约 11:45）表面上的延迟往往是上帝为了更大荣耀而设计的神圣计划。
“Therefore many of the Jews who had come to Mary, and who had seen what Jesus did, believed in Him.” 11:45 Apparent delays are often divine designs for greater glory.

当耶稣到达伯大尼时，得知拉撒路已死四天。使者至少需要 1 天走完 20 英里的路程找到耶稣。拉撒路可能在使者出发后不久就去世了，并根据犹太规矩在当天安葬。因此，耶稣知道拉撒路已经死了，并没有急于动身。
When Jesus reached Bethany, He was told that it had been 4 days since Lazarus died. Messengers take at least 1 day to make the 20 mile journey and to find Jesus. Lazarus probably dies soon after they depart, and is buried the same day according to the Jewish regulation. Thus there was no hurry to go, as Jesus knew Lazarus was already dead.

拉撒路下葬四天后，正是施行叫死人复活奇迹的最佳时机。为什么？根据犹太信仰，死者的灵魂在死后会在身体周围徘徊 3 天。3 天后灵魂离开，身体开始腐烂。如果在 3 天内复活，犹太人会声称这不算神迹，因为灵只是在规定时间内回到了身体。
It was 4 days since Lazarus was buried, and this was a perfect time to perform the miracle of raising Lazarus from the dead. Why? According to Jewish belief the soul of the departed person hung around the body for 3 days after it’s death. The soul would depart after 3 days and then the body would start decaying.

到耶稣到达伯大尼时，4 天已经过去，尸体已经开始腐烂，臭味显而易见。耶稣正在展示他超越死亡的权力和权威：即使是死人也能从坟墓中复活，即使在身体已经腐烂分解之后。即便有一天我们化为尘土、无处可寻，我们也将通过耶稣复活的力量而复活，而不仅仅是苏醒。
If the raising was performed within those 3 days, the Jews would have claimed that there was no miracle involved, as the spirit had simply returned to the body within the requisite time. By the time Jesus reached Bethany, 4 days had passed and the body had started decomposing, evident by the stink and the bad odour. Jesus was demonstrating his power and authority over death, that even a dead person could be raised from the grave, even after rot and decomposition had set in the body, even one day we become dust and dust no where, we will be raised by Jesus’ power of resurrection, not just resuscitation.

耶稣使不可逆转的死亡复活，证明他是“复活与生命”，而不仅仅是一个医治者。这种有目的的延迟是必要的，耶稣通过等待来最大化上帝壮丽的荣耀。
Jesus has resurrected an irreversible death, proving he was the "resurrection and the life" rather than just a healer. The purposeful delay was necessary for Jesus to wait to maximize the spectacular glory of God.

请保持耐心。请保持警觉。我们永远无法完全理解上帝，以及他主权且不可预测的计划。上帝有他的理由为什么我们失去了代理牧师，上帝也有他完美的时机我们将迎来下一位代理牧师。我们永远不该抱怨，不该发怨言。奉耶稣的名，阿们。
Please be patient. Please be vigilant. We can never comprehend fully God, and His sovereign and unpredictable plan. God has his reasons why we lost our vicar, God has His perfect timing when we will have the next vicar. We should never complain. We should never grumble. In Jesus Name, Amen.
